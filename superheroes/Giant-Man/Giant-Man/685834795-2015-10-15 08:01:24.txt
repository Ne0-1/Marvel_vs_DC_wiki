'''Giant-Man''' is the alias used by a number of characters in [[Marvel Comics]].

==Fictional character biography==

===Hank Pym===
'''[[Hank Pym]]''' was the original character named Giant-Man. He has also used other aliases like [[Ant-Man]] and [[Yellowjacket (Marvel Comics)|Yellowjacket]].

===Bill Foster===
'''[[Bill Foster (comics)|Bill Foster]]''' was Hank Pym's successor who originally went by the name Black Goliath.

==Other versions==

===Ultimate Marvel===
In the [[Ultimate Marvel]] universe, there is a group of characters called '''Giant-Men''' who gained size-shifting powers from a modified version of the technology that gave Hank Pym his powers and special jumpsuits that can grow with them. The Giant-Men are part of S.H.I.E.L.D.'s Reserves and consist of [[Ant-Man (Scott Lang)|Scott Lang]], David Scotty, Peter, [[Cassandra Lang]], and some unnamed Giant-Men and Giant-Women.<ref>''Ultimates 2'' #6</ref>

The Giant-Men and the Rocket Men join [[Ultimate Nick Fury|Nick Fury]], [[Quicksilver (comics)|Quicksilver]], and [[Scarlet Witch]] into fighting the Liberators.<ref>''Ultimates 2'' #9</ref>

During the ''[[Ultimatum (Ultimate Marvel)|Ultimatum]]'' storyline, the Giant-Men were seen saving as many people as they can after [[Magneto (comics)|Magneto]] caused a tidal wave that hit Manhattan.<ref>''Ultimatum'' #2</ref>

The Giant-Men later carry the Ultimates away from the forces of [[Loki (comics)|Loki]].<ref>''Ultimate Comics New Ultimates'' #3</ref>

The Giant-Men later attack the West Coast Ultimates and easily defeat them.<ref>''Ultimate Comics Ultimates #11</ref>
