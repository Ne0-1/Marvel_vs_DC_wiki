'''Scientist Supreme''' is a name used by different fictional characters appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].

==Description==
The Scientist Supreme is often described to be the top leader of [[Advanced Idea Mechanics|A.I.M.]], and can also be described to be the scientific counterpart to Earth's Sorcerer Supreme.

==Known versions==
===Yandroth===
{{main article|Yandroth}}
Yandroth was the humanoid Scientist Supreme of the otherdimensional planet Yann, and a counterpart to [[Doctor Strange]]'s title of [[Sorcerer Supreme]].<ref>''Strange Tales'' Vol. 1 #164 (January 1968)</ref>

===Hank Pym===
{{main article|Hank Pym}}
At one point, Hank Pym grew to a large size enought where he encountered the cosmic entity [[Eternity (comics)|Eternity]], claiming to the former that he is Earth's "Scientist Supreme".<ref>''Mighty Avengers'' #30 (October 2009)</ref> Eventually the Eternity's look-a-like quickly identifies himself as actually [[Loki (comics)|Loki]], using the Eternity's form to communicate in order to trick the so-called Scientist Supreme.<ref>''Mighty Avengers'' #34 (March 2010)</ref>

==In other media==
===Television===
* The Lyle Getz version of Scientist Supreme appears in ''[[Iron Man: Armored Adventures]]''. In the episode "Ready, A.I.M., Fire", he was shown to be among the A.I.M. agents working on [[MODOK]].
* The Lyle Getz version of Scientist Supreme appears on ''[[The Avengers: Earth's Mightiest Heroes]]'', voiced by [[Nolan North]] (in "Breakout" Pt. 1 and "Alone Against A.I.M."),<ref>{{cite web|title=Full cast and crew of ''The Avengers: Earth's Mightiest Heroes'' Breakout: Part 1|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt1699027fullcredits#cast |work=[[IMDB]]|accessdate=2011-12-01}}</ref> and by [[Kyle Hebert]] (in "Prisoner of War" and "Secret Invasion").<ref>{{cite web|title=Full cast and crew of ''The Avengers: Earth's Mightiest Heroes'' Prisoner of War|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt2355209fullcredits#cast |work=[[IMDB]]|accessdate=2012-01-07}}</ref> This version is initially comes off like the unknown iteration. In "Breakout" Pt. 1, he has a deal with [[Lucia von Bardas]] (on [[Doctor Doom]]'s behalf) which gets interrupted by [[Iron Man]]. He gets transferred to the [[Vault (comics)|Vault]] only just when the prison breakouts occur, escaping alongside other villains. In "Alone Against A.I.M", the Scientist Supreme gets his revenge by unleashing [[Technovore]] within [[Stark Industries]]. He also holds [[Pepper Potts]] hostage until he's defeated by the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]]. In "Prisoner of War", Getz gets captured by a Skrull impersonator. Although imprisoned, he assists [[Captain America]] and other captured individuals (consisting of [[Invisible Woman]], [[Cobra (Marvel Comics)|Cobra]], [[Viper (Madame Hydra)|Madame Hydra]], [[Clay Quartermain]] and [[Henry Peter Gyrich]]) in escaping from the [[Skrull]]s and [[Super-Skrull]]. 
* The George Clinton version of Scientist Supreme appears in ''[[Avengers Assemble (TV series)|Avengers: Ultron Revolution]]'', voiced by [[Jim Meskimen]].<ref>{{cite episode|title=Adapting to Change|series=Avengers: Ultron Revolution|serieslink=Avengers Assemble (TV series)|network=Disney XD|season=3|number=1|airdate=March 13, 2016}}</ref> This version has the Valdemar Tykkio iteration's accent and [[Andrew Forson]]'s attire. In the episode "Adapting to Change", he has three advanced [[Super-Adaptoid|Adaptoid]]s fight the Avengers. The Scientist Supreme then merges with the three Adaptoids to be the '''[[Super-Adaptoid|Supreme Adaptoid]]''', resulting in a more challenging fight but eventually ends up defeated by the Avengers. However, [[Ultron]]'s salvaged duplicate absorbs Clinton's intelligence and Adaptoid remains, presumably killing the Scientist Supreme in the process. 

===Video games===
* A version of A.I.M's Scientist Supreme appears in ''[[Marvel Avengers Academy]]''. While he is the leader of A.I.M., he is also the headmaster of the A.I.M. Institute of Super-Technology.
