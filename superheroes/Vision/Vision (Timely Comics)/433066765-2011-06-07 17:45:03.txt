'''The Vision''' ('''Aarkus''') is a [[fictional character|fictional]] [[comic book]] [[superhero]] in the [[Marvel Comics]] [[Marvel universe|universe]] who appeared during the 1930-1940s period fans and historians call the [[Golden Age of comic books]]. Created by the writer-artist team of [[Joe Simon]] and [[Jack Kirby]], the character first appeared in ''[[Marvel Mystery Comics]]'' #13 (Nov. 1940), published by Marvel predecessor [[Timely Comics]].

== Publication history ==
The Vision debuted in a four-page feature in ''Marvel Mystery Comics'' #13 (Nov. 1940), and continued as a regular feature in that superhero [[anthology]] through issue #48 (Oct. 1943). He also starred in an eight-page story in ''Kid Comics'' #3 (no month given; previous issue dated Summer 1943).

In modern-day continuity, a [[simulacrum]] of the Vision was temporarily created from the mind of [[Rick Jones (comics)|Rick Jones]], along with those of the [[Angel (Thomas Halloway)|Angel]], the [[Blazing Skull]], the [[Fin (comics)|Fin]], and the [[Jeffrey Mace|Patriot]] to aid the superhero team the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]] during the [[Kree-Skrull War]], in ''The Avengers''  #97 (March 1972). Two decades later, he appeared in a [[Flashback (narrative)|flashback]] story in the [[World War II]]-set superhero-team series ''[[Invaders (comics)|The Invaders]]'' vol. 2, #3 (July 1993). He returns with the other Invaders in the miniseries ''Invaders Now!'', beginning with issue #1 (Nov. 2010).

==Fictional character biography==
The Golden Age Vision, also known as Aarkus, is an alien [[law enforcement officer]] from a dimension called Smokeworld. While Aarkus is looking for a suitable place to exile a prisoner, he is accidentally contacted by an Earth scientist named Markham Erickson. Leaving the prisoner on the planet Jupiter, Aarkus accepts Erickson's invitation to fight crime on Earth.<ref>''[[Marvel Mystery Comics]]'' #13 (Nov. 1940)</ref> Aarkus does so for several years, although during World War II is briefly manipulated by the [[Axis powers of World War II|Axis]] powers into fighting the [[Allies]]. The superhero team the [[Invaders (comics)|Invaders]], however, manage to help Aarkus realize his mistake.<ref>''[[Invaders (comics)|The Invaders]]'' vol. 2, #3 (July 1993)</ref> 

A [[simulacrum]] of the Vision was temporarily created from the mind of [[Rick Jones (comics)|Rick Jones]], along with those of the [[Blazing Skull]], the [[Fin (comics)|Fin]], and the Golden Age [[Angel (Thomas Halloway)|Angel]] and [[Jeffrey Mace|Patriot]], to aid the superhero team the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]] during the [[Kree-Skrull War]].<ref>''[[Avengers (comics)|The Avengers'' vol. 1, #97 (March 1972)]]</ref>

In the present day, it is revealed that he inhabits the sewers under New York, taking care of a Cosmic Cube that is feeding off the feelings of people affected by the death of [[Captain America]], at the end of a stint with time traveling, we last see him with the newly resurrected [[Toro (comics)|Toro]].<ref>''Torch'' # 1</ref>

== Powers and abilities==
The Vision can generate illusory images of himself; fly; generate ice and extreme cold; and teleport wherever there is smoke.
