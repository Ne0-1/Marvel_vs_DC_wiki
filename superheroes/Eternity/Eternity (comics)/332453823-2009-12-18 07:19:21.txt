'''Eternity''' is a [[fictional character]] that appears in [[comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. Created by scripter-editor [[Stan Lee]] and artist-plotter [[Steve Ditko]], the character is first mentioned in ''[[Strange Tales]]'' #134 (July 1965) and [[first appearance|first appears]] in ''Strange Tales'' #138 (Nov. 1965). 

Debuting in the [[Silver Age of Comic Books]], the character appeared in four decades of Marvel continuity and appeared in associated Marvel merchandise including [[animated]] [[television series]]; [[trading cards]] and [[video game]]s.

==Publication history==
Eternity debuted in an epic fifteen issue storyline in ''[[Strange Tales]]'' #130-146 (March 1965 - July 1966), aiding [[mystic]] hero [[Doctor Strange]] against the villain [[Dormammu]]. The character continued to appear in Doctor Strange stories that were cosmic in scope, notably in ''Doctor Strange'' #180 - 182 (May-July 1969) and  ''Doctor Strange'' vol. 2, #10-13 (Oct. 1974 - April 1975).

==Biography==
The superhero team the [[Defenders (comics)|Defenders]] assist the entity by finding three missing fragments of its being and convincing them to rejoin with Eternity.<ref>''Defenders'' #92 (Feb. 1981)</ref> The character appears with the other members of the cosmic pantheon in the [[Parallel universe (fiction)|alternate universe]] ''[[What If (comics)|What If?]]'',<ref>''What If?'' #32 (Apr. 1982)</ref> and the title ''[[Fantastic Four]]'' during the [[trial]] of hero [[Mister Fantastic]], who saved the life of the planet-devourer [[Galactus]] (thus allowing the entity to continue consuming worlds and cause the death of billions of life forms). Eternity allows all present to momentarily become one with the universe, thereby allowing them to understand that Galactus is a vital part of the universe.<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #262 (Jan. 1984)</ref>

The character reappears briefly in the ''[[What If (comics)|What If?]]'';<ref>''What If?'' #43 (Feb. 1984)</ref> in the [[limited series]] ''[[Secret Wars II]]'';<ref>''Secret Wars II'' #6-7 (Dec.-Jan. 1985)</ref> the title ''[[Marvel Saga]]''<ref>''Marvel Saga'' #21 (Aug. 1987)</ref> and the third volume of the ''[[Silver Surfer]]'' during a storyline involving the [[Elders of the Universe]].<ref>''Silver Surfer'' vol. 3, #6 (Dec. 1987) & #10 (Apr. 1988)</ref> Eternity makes a [[cameo appearance]] in an issue of the second volume of ''What If?'';<ref>''What If?'' vol. 2, #1 (July 1989)</ref> the title ''[[Guardians of the Galaxy]]''<ref>''Guardians of the Galaxy'' #16 (Dec. 1991)</ref> and opposes the Titan [[Thanos]] in the limited series ''[[The Infinity Gauntlet]]''.<ref>''The Infinity Gauntlet'' #1-6 (July - Dec. 1991)</ref> 

The title ''[[Quasar (comics)|Quasar]]'' establishes that together with fellow entity [[Infinity (Marvel Comics)|Infinity]], the pair represent the space-time continuum and the living force of the universe,<ref>Revealed in ''Quasar'' #19-25 (Feb.-Aug. 1991)</ref> and that Eternity has spawned several "children", or concepts that became separate, independent entities. These entities are Empathy; Eulogy; Expediency; [[Entropy (comics)|Entropy]]; Epiphany; Enmity and [[Eon (comics)|Eon]]. Eon is eventually killed and replaced in turn by the concept [[Epoch (Marvel Comics)|Epoch]].<ref>''Quasar'' #23 (June 1991)</ref>

Eternity continues to feature in storylines involving the ''[[Infinity Gems]]'', featuring prominently in the title ''Warlock and the Infinity Watch'';<ref>''Warlock and the Infinity Watch'' #1 (Feb. 1992); #9 (Oct. 1992); #11 (Dec. 1992); #14-15 (March-April  1993); #19-20 (Aug.-Sept. 1993)</ref> ''Guardians of the Galaxy''<ref>''Guardians of the Galaxy'' #25 (June 1992); #35 (April 1993); #44 (Jan. 1994); #46 (March 1994); #49 (June 1994)</ref> and features a major plot device in the limited series ''[[Infinity War]]'', with an appearance in the title ''Quasar''.<ref>''Quasar'' #38 (Sept. 1992)</ref> Eternity is initially imprisoned by the evil [[alter ego]] of [[Adam Warlock]], the [[Magus (comics)|Magus]], who wishes to reunite the Infinity Gems for his own use. The villain is eventually defeated when Eternity merges with Infinity and together they strip the Magus of his newfound power.<ref>''Infinity War'' #1-6 (June-Nov. 1992)</ref>

The character appears briefly the limited series ''[[Infinity Crusade]]'';<ref>''Infinity Crusade'' #1 - 6 (June - Nov. 1993)</ref> ''The Warlock Chronicles''<ref>''The Warlock Chronicles'' #1 - 8 (July 1993 - Feb. 1994)</ref> and the ''Thor Corps''.<ref>''Thor Corps'' #1 - 4 (Sep. - Dec. 1993)</ref> After a humorous appearance in the ''Marvel Holiday Special 1994''<ref>''Marvel Holiday Special 1994'' (Jan. 1995)</ref> the character features heavily in the limited series ''[[Avengers Infinity]]'', being summoned by a team of the ''[[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]]'' to reason with the cosmic entities the ''Infinites''.<ref>''Avengers Infinity'' #1 - 4 (Sep. - Dec. 2000)</ref> After an appearance in a ''[[Fantastic Four]]'' annual and the third ongoing volume of the title involving the appearance of cosmic entity [[Abraxas]],<ref>''Fantastic Four'' Annual 2001 (Jan. 2001) & ''Fantastic Four'' vol. 3, #49 (Jan. 2002)</ref> the character appears in the limited series ''[[Infinity Abyss]]'', and observes the attempts of Thanos and several of Earth's heroes to defeat several of his [[cloning|clone]]s, who are dedicated to destroying the universe.<ref>''Infinity Abyss'' #1 - 2 (Aug. 2002); #3 - 4 (Sep. 2002); #5 - 6 (Oct. 2002)</ref>

Eternity appears in the fifth volume of the title ''[[Captain Marvel (Marvel Comics)|Captain Marvel]]'' witnesses the growing insanity of the hero;<ref>''Captain Marvel'' vol. 5, #5-6 (March- April 2003); #18 (March 2004)</ref> and in the limited series ''[[Marvel: The End]]'' is one of the last beings to surrender to Thanos when he uses the [[Magic item|artifact]] the [[Heart of the Universe]] to undo the universe and then remake it minus a fatal flaw.<ref>''Marvel: The End'' #1-2 (May 2003); #3-4 (June 2003) & #5 (July 2003) & #6 (Aug. 2003)</ref> The character is effected by the machinations of the villain [[Krona (comics)|Krona]] when engineers a merging of the [[Marvel Universe]] and the [[DC Universe]] in the limited series ''[[JLA/Avengers]]''<ref>''JLA/Avengers #1 (Sept. 2003) & 3 (Dec. 2003); ''Avengers/JLA'' #2 (Oct. 2003) & 4 (May 2004)</ref> and is threatened in the third volume of the ''Defenders'' when Dormammu unsuccessfully attempts to invade the [[Earth-616]] universe and remake it in his image.<ref>Defenders vol. 3, #1-5 (Sept. 2005 - Jan. 2006)</ref>

Eternity briefly appears in the title ''Incredible Hercules'' when protagonist [[Hercules (Marvel Comics)|Hercules]] and his allies are en route to battle the [[Skrull]] [[god]]s as part of the companywide "[[Secret Invasion]]" storyline.<ref>''Incredible Hercules'' #118 (Aug. 2008); ''Secret Invasion'' #1-8 (June 2008 - Jan. 2009)</ref>

Eternity appears during the [[Dark Reign (comics)|Dark Reign]] storyline, advising [[Henry Pym]] that he is the 'Scientist Supreme of Earth', and founder of future teams of ''[[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]]''.<ref>''Mighty Avengers'' #30 (Dec. 2009)</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Eternity is an abstract entity, but can warp surrounding space and matter into a manifestation that can be perceived by lesser beings. The character can also manipulate the universe to achieve virtually any effect desired, and exists everywhere simultaneously. Eternity can also form [[avatar]]s from another plane of existence known as the Dimension of Manifestations.<ref>''Quasar'' #37 (Aug. 1992)</ref>

==Other media==
Eternity makes a [[cameo appearance]] in an episode of ''[[X-Men (TV series)|X-Men: The Animated Series]]'' (1992) and appears semi-regularly in the [[animated]] ''[[Silver Surfer (TV series)|Silver Surfer]]'' series, (1994) voiced by [[John Neville (actor)|John Neville]].
