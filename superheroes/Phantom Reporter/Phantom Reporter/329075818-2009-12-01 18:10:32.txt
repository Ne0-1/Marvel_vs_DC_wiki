The '''Phantom Reporter''' ('''Richard "Dick" Jones''') is a [[Fictional character|fictional]] [[comic book]] character who first appeared in ''[[Daring Mystery Comics]]'' #3, published by [[Timely Comics]], the forerunner to [[Marvel Comics]], during the period known to fans and historians as the [[Golden Age of Comic Books]]. 

He will reappear in ''[[The Twelve (comic)|The Twelve]]''.<ref>[http://www.comicbookresources.com/news/newsitem.cgi?id=11312 Mystery Men's Dozen: Brevoort Talks "The Twelve"], July 26, 2007, [[Comic Book Resources]]</ref> <ref>[http://forum.newsarama.com/showthread.php?t=125392 12 Days of the Twelve: The Phantom Reporter], August 15, 2007, [[Newsarama]]</ref> He had no known superpowers. His one Golden Age story has been reprinted in ''The Twelve'' #0.

==Fictional character biography==
{{Unreferenced section|date=November 2009}}
Dick Jones was ex-All American fullback, ex-collegiate boxing, wrestling and fencing champ. He actually has three identities: Dick Jones, reporter; Van Engen, millionaire; and Phantom Reporter, costumed crime fighter.

===The Twelve===
{{Main|The Twelve (comics)}}
After being gassed, cryonically-frozen, and discovered during a construction project, he found himself in the year 2008 and dealt with the modern world. As of the first issues, Phantom Reporter struggles to deal with his growing sexual attraction to [[Black Widow (Timely Comics)|Black Widow]], who was also recently awaken in the year 2008. 

Soon, he is offered a job with the ''[[Daily Bugle]]''. For the editors at the ''Bugle'', the Phantom Reporter's first-hand experience of America's past — specifically all the good and ill that was present — will be invaluable to their readers. He accepts the job, writing his first article on the sacrifice and patriotism of his generation of heroes.
