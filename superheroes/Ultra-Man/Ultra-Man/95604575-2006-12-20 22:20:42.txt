'''Gary Concord, the Ultra-Man''' is a [[fictional character]], a [[Golden Age of comic books|Golden Age]] [[superhero]] from [[All-American Publications]], which merged in 1946 with DC Comics-predecessor [[National Periodical Publications]].

He is a separate [[DC Universe]] character from [[Ultraman (comics)|Ultraman]], a supervillain and  evil counterpart of [[Superman]], who was introduced in ''[[Justice League of America]]'' #29 (Aug. 1964).

Created by [[writer]]-[[artist]] [[Jon L. Blummer]] under the [[pseudonym]] Don Shelby, Ultra-Man [[first appearance|first appeared]] in All-American's flagship title, ''[[All-American Comics]]'' #8 (Nov. 1939), in the first part of a two-part story. Part one featured Gary Concord Sr., a 20th-century scientist who through [[suspended animation]] awakened as a chemically altered superhuman in the year 2174. Part two, in ''All-American Comics'' #9 (Dec. 1939), continues the story with Gary Concord Jr., born in 2214 to the lifespan-lengthened Gary Sr. and wife Leandra. After Gary Sr. died of natural causes in 2239, Gary Jr. took over as Ultra-Man.  He would appear until issue #19 in 1940.

The latter Ultra-Man made a [[flashback (literary technique)|flashback]] appearance in "The Last Earth-Prime Story", in ''[[Superman (comic book)|Superman]]'' #411 (Sept. 1985), and in ''Legionnaires Annual'' #3 (1996), in which he and [[Avatar (comics)|Avatar]], [[Behemoth (comics)|Behemoth]], and [[Metallica (comics)|Metallica]] formed a 100th-century [[Legion of Super-Heroes]] on the planet Almeer-5.
