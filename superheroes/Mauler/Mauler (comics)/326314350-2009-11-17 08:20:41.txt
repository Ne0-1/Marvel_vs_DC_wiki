'''Mauler''' (an [[acronym]] for '''M'''obile '''A'''rmored '''U'''tility '''L'''aser-guided '''E'''-beam, '''R'''evised) is a name used by two [[fictional characters]] that appear in [[comic books]] published by [[Marvel Comics]]. The first version appears in ''[[Daredevil (Marvel Comics)|Daredevil]]'' #167 (Nov. 1980) and was created by [[David Michelinie]] and [[Frank Miller]]. The second character first appears in ''[[Iron Man]]'' #156 (Mar. 1982) and was created by [[David Michelinie]]; [[John Romita, Jr.]] and [[Pablo Marcos]]. 

==Publication history==
'''Aaron Soames''' first appears as the Mauler in the title ''Daredevil'',<ref>''Daredevil'' #167 (Nov. 1980)</ref> and is revealed to be an elderly, former [[employee]] of Cord Conglomerate deprived of his [[pension]] benefits by a computer error. Soames steals the [[prototype]] suit of Mauler armor in the hopes of punishing [[Edwin Cord]], who is indifferent to Soames' plight. The character has two skirmishes with the hero Daredevil, and after humiliating Cord is killed with advanced weaponry by Cord's men during the second encounter. The Mauler armor reappears in the title when small-time criminal Turk Barrett steals the armor and attempts to kill Daredevil. Barrett, however, is defeated in seconds.<ref>''Daredevil'' #176 (Nov. 1981)</ref>

[[Mercenary]]''' Brendan Doyle''' appears as the Mauler for the first time in the title ''[[Iron Man]]''.<ref>''Iron Man'' #156 (March 1982)</ref> Doyle is hired by the now imprisoned Edwin Cord to steal the armor from [[Stark International]] (the company of Iron Man's [[alter ego]], [[Tony Stark]]) and destroy all records of the suit design and history. Although successful in stealing the suit, Doyle is prevented from reaching the records by former comrade [[War Machine|Jim Rhodes]]. As a soldier for hire, the character appears in ''[[Marvel Team-Up]]'' against heroes [[Spider-Man]] and [[Wonder Man]]<ref>''Marvel Team-Up'' #136 (Dec. 1983)</ref> and encounters Spider-Man once again while trying to retrieve his infant son in ''Spectacular Spider-Man''.<ref>''Spectacular Spider-Man'' #122 (Jan. 1987)</ref> Doyle briefly appears during the [[Armor Wars]] storyline in the title ''Iron Man'', when the Mauler armour [[confiscated]] by the hero (who is tracking down [[technology]] based on his stolen designs).<ref>''Iron Man'' #225 (Dec. 1987)</ref>

Doyle reappears as the Mauler in the [[Parallel universe (fiction)|alternate universe]] title ''[[What If (comics)|What If?]]''<ref>''What If?'' #8 (Dec. 1989)</ref> and with a new suit of Mauler armor in ''[[Alpha Flight]]''.<ref>''Alpha Flight'' #113 (Oct. 1992)</ref> Jim Rhodes briefly impersonates the Mauler in an ''Iron Man'' Annual.<ref>''Iron Man'' Annual #16 (1995)</ref> The character features briefly in the [[one-shot]] publication ''Iron Man: The Legend''<ref>''Iron Man: The Legend'' #1 (Sep. 1996)</ref> and in the [[Civil War (comics)|Civil War]] storyline<ref>''Civil War: War Crimes'' #1 (Feb. 2007)</ref>  before featuring in the title ''Avengers: The Initiative''.<ref>''Avengers: The Initiative'' #13 (July 2008)</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
The Mauler armor provides heavy protection from physical and energy-based attacks, boosts the wearer's strength and courtesy of [[turbines]] allows [[flight]]. In addition to internal life support systems, a [[laser]] cannon that doubles as an [[electron]] [[Particle beam|particle gun]] is mounted on the left arm. The right palm of the armor can also generate a high-frequency [[electric shock]].
