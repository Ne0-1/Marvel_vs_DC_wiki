'''Solo''' ('''James Bourne''') is a [[fictional character]], appearing in comic books by [[Marvel Comics]].

==Publication history==
Solo first appeared in ''Web of Spider-Man'' #19 (October 1986). He was created by writer [[David Michelinie]] and artist [[Marc Silvestri]]. Solo has had his own self-titled limited series.

==Fictional character biography==
Born in the USA but having renounced his citizenship to any country, Solo works as a counter-terrorism operative, and makes limited appearances in the Marvel Comics universe. He is known for his [[catchphrase]], "While Solo lives, terror dies!"

In his first appearance, Solo teleported inside a foreign embassy in West Germany and killed all the terrorists inside.<ref>''Web of Spider-Man'' #19</ref>  He next foiled [[ULTIMATUM (comics)|ULTIMATUM]]'s plot to destroy the [[Arc de Triomphe]] in Paris.<ref>''Amazing Spider-Man'' #323</ref> He also shot ULTIMATUM terrorists who were trying to destroy Ellis Island, and then joined forces with [[Spider-Man]] to capture ULTIMATUM's commanding officer.<ref>''Amazing Spider-Man'' #324</ref> Outside [[Barcelona]], Spain, Solo assassinated Toro Mendoza, leader of the Cascan separatists.<ref>''Amazing Spider-Man Annual'' #24</ref>

When the [[Sinister Six]] reform and defeat both Spider-Man and the [[Hulk (comics)|Hulk]], Solo aids the web-slinger in his second fight against the super villain team. Things take a turn for the worse when [[Mysterio]] uses an illusion to make Solo believe he is attacking the Six, when in reality he is attacking Spider-Man while the Six escape.<ref>''Spider-Man'' #20-23</ref> Solo escapes and Spider-Man is rescued by [[Crimson Commando|Cyborg X]] and [[Deathlok]].{{Issue|date=April 2009}} Soon after, Solo rejoins Spider-Man to aid him in a final assault on the Six aided by the Hulk, [[Ghost Rider (Danny Ketch)|Ghost Rider]], [[Sleepwalker (comics)|Sleepwalker]], [[Nova (Richard Rider)|Nova]], Deathlok and the [[Fantastic Four]]. With the villains defeated, Solo disappears.{{Issue|date=April 2009}}

Solo later fought [[Tarantula (Marvel Comics)#Luis Alvarez|La Tarantula]].<ref>''Marvel Comics Presents'' #88</ref> He defeated a Sicilian crime syndicate,<ref>''Amazing Spider-Man Annual'' #26</ref> and then joined forces with Spider-Man against the agents of the [[Taskmaster]] and the [[Red Skull]].<ref>''Amazing Spider-Man'' #367</ref> A rematch with La Tarantula ended with each combatant wrongly believing he had killed the other.<ref>''Marvel Comics Presents'' #124</ref> Solo then joined forces with [[Black Cat (comics)|Black Cat]] against the terrorist organization called ARES, and stopped their money laundering operation. He encountered their leader Deathstorm who revealed he had ties with Solo's past.<ref>''Amazing Spider-Man Annual'' #27</ref> Solo later joined forces with [[Nick Fury]] against the [[Viper (Madame Hydra)|Viper]].<ref>''SHIELD'' #44-45</ref>

Solo also once helped Spider-Man take on criminally-trained specialist versions of [[Captain America]], [[Hawkeye (comics)|Hawkeye]] and Spider-Man himself.<ref>''Amazing Spider-Man'' #367</ref>

Later, Solo assists dozens of other heroes in battling a seemingly rampaging Wolverine (he was being mentally influenced). He confronts Wolverine, side-by-side with [[Cardiac (comics)|Cardiac]]. Solo is swiftly defeated, suffering deep lacerations in the process. Cardiac is subdued with masonry.{{Issue|date=April 2009}}

Solo is hired by [[G. W. Bridge]] to join the new [[Six Pack (comics)|Six Pack]] alongside Hammer, [[Domino (comics)|Domino]], [[Anaconda (comics)|Anaconda]], and [[Constrictor (comics)|Constrictor]] in their mission to take down [[Cable (comics)|Cable]]. Like Bridge and Hammer, Solo is captured and placed in suspended animation. He is eventually released.<ref>''[[Cable & Deadpool]]'' #7-12</ref>

===Civil War===
During the [[Civil War (comic book)|Civil War]], Solo sides with other heroes who oppose registration, including [[Battlestar (comics)|Battlestar]] and [[Typeface (comics)|Typeface]]. While waiting to make contact with the resistance led by [[Captain America]], Solo and the others are arrested by [[Iron Man]], [[Ms. Marvel]], [[Wonder Man]] and [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]] agents.<ref>''Civil War'' #6</ref>

Solo has been identified as one of the 142 registered superheroes.<ref>''[[Avengers: The Initiative]]'' #1</ref><ref>[http://www.marvel.com/news/comicstories.947 ''Avengers: The Initiative'' #1 Character Map]</ref>
Solo was hired along with Clay, as bodyguards to protect Wally and Molly, cheery twins who sing Mutant hate-songs. They chained up [[M (comics)|M]], tied up [[Siryn]] and taped her mouth shut, and locked both women in a desert bunker after they tried to round two kids up in order to enforce the kids grandparents' court granted visitation rights.{{Issue|date=April 2009}}

===Dark Reign===
Solo was hired by a man later revealed to be [[Bullseye (comics)|Bullseye]]'s father to capture Bullseye. Bullseye threw a shard of glass into Solo's left eye, but Solo survived and managed to capture Bullseye after tasering him.<ref>''Dark Reign: Hawkeye'' #4</ref> The assassins chasing [[Elektra (comics)|Elektra]] attempt to hire Solo to assist but fail.<ref>''Dark Reign: Elektra'' #4-5 (March 2009)</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Solo has somewhat limited [[teleportation]] abilities, allowing him to "jump" from place to place for short distances; there appears to be a limit to the distance and the amount of time between Solo's teleports. He has a gifted intellect, and is a master of many forms of hand-to-hand combat. He is highly adept in the use of conventional weapons and firearms and is an expert marksman.

Solo wears bullet-proof quilted Kevlar with pouches to hold weapons and ammunition. He carries an arsenal of portable conventional weaponry, including sub-machine guns, automatic rifles, automatic pistols, hand grenades, combat knives, etc., and has been known to use ninja climbing claws.
