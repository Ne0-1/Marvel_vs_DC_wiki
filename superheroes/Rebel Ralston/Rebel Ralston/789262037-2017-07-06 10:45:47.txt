'''Robert''' "'''Rebel'''" '''Ralston''' is a [[fictional character]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. The character is known for appearing in ''[[Sgt. Fury and his Howling Commandos]]''.

==Publication history==
{{expand-section|date=February 2015}}
Rebel Ralston first appeared in ''[[Sgt. Fury and his Howling Commandos]]'' #1 (May 1963) and was created by [[Stan Lee]], [[Jack Kirby]], and [[Dick Ayers]].

==Fictional character biography==
Robert "Rebel" Ralston is a native of [[Kentucky]] and a founding member of the original [[Sgt. Fury and his Howling Commandos|Howling Commandos]] led by [[Nick Fury]]. Rebel Ralston fought alongside the team during [[World War II]].<ref>"Captain America: White" #2-5 (2016)</ref> Ralston was an accomplished [[Equestrianism|horseman]] and was skilled with a [[lasso]]. He was also the "creator" of the Howlers battle cry "Wahoo!".{{issue|date=April 2013}} Mr. Ralston and Mrs. Ralston were seen.<ref>''Sgt. Fury and his Howling Commandos'' #24</ref>

After the war, he was one of the founding members of the [[V-Battalion]] and the [[Penance Council]].{{Issue|date=January 2010}} He was later elected [[United States Senate|Senator]] of [[Texas]], apparently with clandestine help from the V-Battalion.<ref>''Citizen V and the V Battalion #2''</ref> He rejoined his old comrades in the Howling Commandos to battle a Baron Strucker robot. The Commandos and [[Captain America]] stopped the Strucker robot but the Howling Commandos' superior [[Happy Sam Sawyer|Captain Sam Sawyer]] was killed.{{issue|date=April 2013}} He survived an assassination attempt while co-chairing the Senate Defense Committee.{{issue|date=April 2013}} He has worked closely with S.H.I.E.L.D. and the Commission on Superhuman Activities.<ref>''The Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe A-Z'' Vol. 9</ref>

When Fury was seemingly killed by [[Punisher]], Ralston and [[Iron Man]] advised the President not to attend the funeral in the event that the supervillains attack it.<ref>''Incredible Hulk'' #434</ref>

In the form of Lt. Dallas, Omnibus placed Ralston under mental control to turn over information that can used to fund a terrorist organization called "The Alliance".<ref>''Incredible Hulk'' #436</ref> When the [[Hulk (comics)|Hulk]] was captured by Major [[Glenn Talbot]], Ralston observed Bruce Banner in custody and refused to have Talbot kill Banner until the President has made a decision. Robert attended a briefing on "The Alliance" with Talbot, [[Dum Dum Dugan]], [[Valentina Allegra de Fontaine]] and [[Henry Peter Gyrich]].<ref>''Incredible Hulk'' #437</ref> The Senator and his allies continued to debate about "The Alliance". They could not determine who "The Alliance" were and what their motive is.<ref>''Incredible Hulk'' #439</ref> After Hulk had claimed responsibility for "The Alliance" in order to serve as an enemy that the people can fight back against, [[Thor (Marvel Comics)|Thor]] confronted Ralston and the others stating that he will confront Hulk.<ref>''Incredible Hulk'' #440</ref>

Robert was contacted by [[J. Jonah Jameson]] as he searched for support against [[Bastion (comics)|Bastion]] and Operation: Zero Tolerance. Ralston suggested to Jameson to try Senator [[Robert Kelly (comics)|Robert Kelly]] who was more frustrated with the mutant issue than him. Jameson frustratingly then states of voting for a Republican by the next election.<ref>''Uncanny X-Men'' #346</ref>

Ralston traveled to Sudan where he met many of his former Howling Commandos teammates (consisting of Nick Fury, Captain America, [[Sharon Carter]] and [[John Garrett (comics)|John Garrett]]) at Fury's Sandbox for one last party together. Fury talked with Ralston for a moment about the support for Nick's operation that would get the U.S. Senate and the Senator only asked how soon Nick will need those votes.<ref>''[[Secret Warriors (2009 series)|Secret Warriors]]'' #17</ref> Robert listed to a good few stories from the past.<ref>''Secret Warriors'' #18</ref> Ralston raised his glass in a toast to the fallen members of the Howling Commanders.<ref>''Secret Warriors'' #19</ref>

Robert spoke to the [[United Nations]] about the President's plans for international funds to provide economic, political, and military stabilities in areas suffering from international terrorist threats. Ralston was appointed control over the fund.<ref>''Secret Warriors'' #27</ref> The Senator picked up [[Jasper Sitwell]] and Dum Dum Dugan from the United Nations' prison and brought them to his limousine waiting for them with Nick and Garrett already inside.<ref>''Secret Warriors'' #28</ref>

As part of the ''[[Marvel NOW!]]'' event, he attended a weapons expo where he met with [[Maria Hill]] and [[Nick Fury, Jr.]] as they noticed the [[Iron Patriot]] armor on display as one of the items at the convention. A fight broke out against [[Advanced Idea Mechanics|A.I.M.]], and he was apparently killed by [[Black Widow (Yelena Belova)|Yelena Belova]] during the fight.<ref>''Secret Avengers'' vol. 2 #3</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
In his prime, Rebel Ralston is a trained ranger where he was usually wielding a machine gun and grenades. Besides being an expert horse rider during his days as a jockey, he was an expert at wielding a lasso.

==Other versions==
The [[Ultimate Marvel]] version of Robert Ralston is seen as a United States Senator. He was present during the [[President of the United States]]'s hearing regarding the Winter Protocols' implementation,<ref>''Ultimate Comics: Ultimates'' vol. 1 #8''</ref> and dies during the [[Mister Fantastic|Maker]]'s subsequent attack.<ref>''Ultimate Comics: Ultimates'' vol. 1 #9</ref>
