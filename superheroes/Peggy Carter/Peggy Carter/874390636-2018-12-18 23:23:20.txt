'''Margaret''' "'''Peggy'''" '''Carter''' is a fictional character appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. She is usually depicted as a supporting character in books featuring [[Captain America]]. Created by writer [[Stan Lee]] and artist [[Jack Kirby]], she first appeared in ''[[Tales of Suspense]]'' #77 as a [[World War II]] love interest of Steve Rogers in [[Flashback (narrative)|flashback]] sequences. She would later be better known as a relative of Captain America's modern-day significant other [[Sharon Carter]].

[[Hayley Atwell]] portrays the character in the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]], beginning with the 2011 film ''[[Captain America: The First Avenger]]'', and continuing in the [[Marvel One-Shot]] ''[[Agent Carter (film)|Agent Carter]]'', the 2014 film ''[[Captain America: The Winter Soldier]]'', the television series ''[[Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.|Marvel's Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.]]'' and ''[[Agent Carter (TV series)|Marvel's Agent Carter]],'' the 2015 films ''[[Avengers: Age of Ultron]],''  ''[[Ant-Man (film)|Ant-Man]]'' and a cameo in the 2016 film ''[[Captain America: Civil War]]''.

==Publication history==
The character first appeared, unnamed, as a wartime love interest of Captain America in ''[[Tales of Suspense]]'' #75 (single panel) and #77 (May 1966), by writer [[Stan Lee]] and artist [[Jack Kirby]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.ign.com/characters/peggy-carter | title=Peggy Carter | publisher=[[IGN]] | accessdate=21 December 2013}}</ref><ref>[http://www.comics.org/issue/20053/#147873 "If a Hostage Should Die!"], ''[[Tales of Suspense]]'' #77 (May 1966) at the [[Grand Comics Database]]</ref> She appeared again as the older sister of [[Sharon Carter]] in ''[[Captain America (comic book)|Captain America]]'' #161 (May 1973). She was later [[retcon]]ned as Sharon's aunt due to the [[floating timeline|unaging nature]] of comic book characters (see ''Captain America'' Vol. 5 #25 (April 2007)). The character has appeared frequently in Captain America stories set during [[World War II]].{{citation needed|date=February 2014}}

An unnamed blonde British agent known as Agent Zero was rescued from Berlin by the [[Young Allies (Marvel Comics)|Young Allies]] after her capture by the [[Red Skull]] and then joins up with Captain America in ''Young Allies'' #1 (Summer 1941).<ref>Reprinted in ''Captain America: Forever Allies'', 2011, New York, Marvel Comics.</ref>

==Fictional character biography==
{{expand section|date=June 2018}}
Peggy Carter joins the [[French Resistance]] as a teenager and becomes a skilled fighter, who serves on several operations alongside Captain America.<ref>Catherine Saunders, Heather Scott, Julia March, and Alastair Dougall, editors, 2008, ''Marvel Chronicle: A year by Year History'', London: Dorling Kindersley, p. 115; {{ISBN|978-1-4093-8399-4}}.</ref> The two fall in love, but an exploding shell gives her amnesia, and she is sent to live with her parents in Virginia.<ref>''Tales of Suspense'' #77. Marvel Comics.</ref>

With Captain America thought dead, she lives a quiet life for many years. After Captain America reemerges in the present day, unaged after having been in [[suspended animation]], Carter encounters the villain [[Doctor Faustus (comics)|Doctor Faustus]], from whom Captain America rescues her.<ref>''Captain America'' #161-162. Marvel Comics.</ref>

==Other versions==
* In the alternate reality created by Scarlet Witch in the 2005 "[[House of M]]" storyline, Captain America is never frozen in the Arctic, and instead marries Peggy shortly after World War II ends.<ref name="CAv5no10">''[[Captain America]]'' vol. 5 #10 (Oct. 2005). Marvel Comics.</ref>
* On [[Spider-Gwen|Earth-65]], Peggy Carter is the long-lived director of S.H.I.E.L.D., much like Nick Fury in the primary universe. She also sports an eye patch similar to the one worn by Fury.<ref>''Spider-Gwen'' #2</ref>
* In ''Super-Soldier: Man of War'' #1 from [[Amalgam Comics]], '''Mademoiselle Peggy''' is a cross between Peggy Carter and [[DC Comics]]' World War II version of [[Mademoiselle Marie]].<ref>''Super-Soldier: Man of War'' #1 (June 1997). Amalgam Comics.</ref>
