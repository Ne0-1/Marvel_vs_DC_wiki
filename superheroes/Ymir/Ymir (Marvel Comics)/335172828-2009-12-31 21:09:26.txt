Based on the frost giant [[Ymir]] from [[Norse mythology]], '''Ymir''' is a [[fictional character]] that appears in [[publications]] published by [[Marvel Comics]]. The character first appears in ''[[Journey Into Mystery]]'' #97 (Oct 1963) and was created by [[Stan Lee]] & [[Jack Kirby]].

==Publication history==
The character Ymir first appears in the title ''[[Journey Into Mystery]]'', and as with the mythological version considers all other forms of life — with the exception of his kin the [[Giants (Marvel Comics)|Frost Giants]] of ''Niffelheim'' — to be abhorations that must be destroyed. The stort depicts an early battle with and subsequent imprisonement by ''[[Odin (Marvel Comics)|Odin]]'', King of the [[Norse gods]].<ref>''Journey Into Mystery'' #97 - 98 (Oct. - Nov. 1963)</ref> Ymir is mentioned in the magazine title ''[[Savage Tales]]'' in a story set in the [[prehistoric]] [[Hyborian Age]]. [[Barbarian]] hero [[Conan (comics)|Conan]] encounters his daughter, Atali, and slays two of her brutish brothers.<ref>''Savage Tales'' #1 (May 1971). An adaption of the [[Robert Ervin Howard|Robert E. Howard]] short story [[The Frost Giant's Daughter]], first published in ''Weird Tales'' (1932).</ref> 

Ymir reappears in a two-part storyline in ''[[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]]'' and ''[[Doctor Strange]]'' being summoned to Earth with the fire demon [[Surtur (comics)|Surtur]] by the cult the [[Sons of Satannish]]. The pair are defeated and banished when the heroes trick the two into fighting one another.<ref>''''Avengers'' #61 (Feb. 1969) & ''Doctor Strange'' #177 (Feb. 1969)</ref> Ymir and Surtur reappear in the title ''[[Thor (Marvel Comics)|Thor]]'', and invade the realm of ''[[Asgard (Marvel Comics)|Asgard]]''. Both characters, however, are defeated when God of Thunder ''Thor'' uses the ''Odinpower'' to banish the pair to the other-dimensional ''Sea of Eternal Night''.<ref>''Thor'' #425 (Oct. 1990)</ref> Ymir reappears in the publication ''[[Marvel Super-Heroes]]'', and with the Frost and Storm Giants launches attack on Asgard. On this occasion the character and his allies are stopped by Thor and his half-brother, [[Vidar (Marvel Comics)|Vidar]].<ref>''Marvel Super-Heroes'' vol. 3, #5 (Apr. 1991)</ref> 

Ymir appears in an issue of the [[limited series]] ''[[X-Men: First Class]]'', which is set during the early days of the modern Marvel universe. The original [[X-Men]] encounter a group called the ''Sons of the Vanir'' who summon Ymir to Earth, and after a brief battle ''Thor'' uses his mystic hammer ''[[Mjolnir (Marvel Comics)|Mjolnir]]'' to return the character to Niffelheim.<ref>''X-Men: First Class'' #5 (Mar. 2007)</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Ymir is a huge ice giant over 1,000 feet (300 m) tall, and possessing immense strength and durability.  Ymir is one of the few being that are capable of fighting Surtur and Odin with a fair chance of actually winning.  The character generate intense, deadly cold and regenerate from as little as a particle of ice. Ymir also carries a huge icicle which functions as a club. The character is an ancient being devoted to destruction and little else.

==Other versions==
===Ultimates===
Ymir and Surtur feature in the ''[[Ultimate Marvel]]'' imprint title ''[[Ultimates]]'', invading Earth and battling the [[Parallel universe (fiction)|alternate universe]] version of the ''Avengers''.<ref>''Ultimates'' vol. 2, #13 (May 2007)</ref>

==In other media==
Ymir appeared in an episode of ''[[Spider-Man and His Amazing Friends]]'' voiced by [[John Stephenson (actor)|John Stephenson]] (1981 - 1983).

The character also appears in the [[video game]] ''[[Marvel: Ultimate Alliance]]'' (2006) voiced by [[Paul Eiding]].
