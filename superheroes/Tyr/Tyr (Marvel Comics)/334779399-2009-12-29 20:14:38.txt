'''Tyr''' is a [[fictional character]] appearing in the [[Marvel Comics]] [[Marvel Universe|universe]], based loosely on the [[Tyr]] of [[Norse mythology|Norse myth]].

==Fictional character biography==
Tyr is the Asgardian god of war. Tyr was renowned throughout [[Asgard (Marvel Comics)|Asgard]] as being the only god brave enough to place his hand in the mouth of the wolf-god [[Fenris Wolf (Marvel Comics)|Fenris]] during the latter's binding; his left hand is missing as a result, having been bitten off by Fenris, and is now usually covered by a metal cup. He has fought in the defense of Asgard numerous times.  He has several times rebelled against [[Odin (Marvel Comics)|Odin]] due to his love of battle and fought [[Thor (Marvel Comics)|Thor]] due to resentment of Thor's relationship to [[Sif (Marvel Comics)|Sif]].

Tyr battled with Asgardian forces against a false Ragnarok.<ref>''Thor'' #275</ref> He later lost a friendly wager to Thor.<ref>''Thor'' #302</ref> Tyr later allied with [[Loki (comics)|Loki]] against Odin, and captured the goddess [[Idunn (Marvel Comics)|Idunn]] and her Golden Apples of Immortality. He unleashed the [[Midgard Serpent (Marvel Comics)|Midgard Serpent]] on Earth. With the enemies of Asgard, he followed Odin and the other gods to Earth, where he was betrayed by Loki and defeated by Odin.<ref>''Thor'' #324-327</ref> Alongside [[Beta Ray Bill]], the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]], the [[Fantastic Four]], and the Asgardian forces, Tyr battled [[Surtur (Marvel Comics)|Surtur]]'s demons on Earth; Tyr then returned to Asgard.<ref>''Thor'' #350-352, 359</ref> He also fought in the defense of Asgard against the Egyptian death-god [[Seth (Marvel Comics)|Seth]].{{Issue|date=May 2009}}

==Powers and abilities==
Tyr is a member of the race of superhumans known as Asgardians, and therefore has superhuman strength, speed, stamina, durability, agility, and reflexes, and has an extremely long life span and immunity to all Earthly diseases. Tyr, being the god of war, is not only highly skilled at hand-to-hand combat but proficient with all forms of Asgardian weaponry as well.

He usually carries a sword and shield. Tyr once stole the Mace of the Myth-Wars, a weapon once wielded by Odin and possessing powers similar to Thor's hammer [[Mjolnir (comics)|Mjolnir]], as well as the ability of teleportation.

==Other Marvel characters named Tyr==
Tyr the Asgardian god is not to be confused with Tyr of the Blinding Blade, an inhabitant of the World with a Thousand Galaxies who appeared in ''Thor Annual'' #2.
