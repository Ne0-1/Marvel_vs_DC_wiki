'''Gladiatrix''' ('''Robin Braxton''') is a fictional [[heroine]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].

==Publication history==
Gladiatrix first appeared in ''[[Thing (comics)|Thing]]''  #33 ''(March 1986)'', and was created by [[Mike Carlin]] and [[Ron Wilson (comics)|Ron Wilson]].

The character subsequently appears in ''Captain America'' #352 (April 1989), #389-392 (August–September 1991), #411-413 (January–March 1993), ''Civil War: Front Line'' #4 (September 2006), ''Amazing Spider-Man'' #535 (November 2006), ''Civil War'' #6-7 (December 2006-January 2007), ''World War Hulk: Front Line'' #4 (November 2007), and ''Civil War Chronicles'' #10 (July 2008).

==Fictional character biography==
Becoming a professional wrestler shortly after her youth, '''Robin Braxton''' has been fighting for her life ever since. She gained her current enhanced strength through an augmentation given by Power Broker INC. Shortly afterward she abandoned her life as a wrestler and became part of the Grapplers.

They were forced to restrain [[Titania (Grapplers)|Titania]], who had just lost a battle from Battleaxe. When the Grapplers came face to face with the Thing, Gladiatrix decided to remain on the good side and helped him out, saving the day.<ref>''Thing'' #33</ref> Later, Gladiatrix tried out for membership in [[Avengers (comics)|the Avengers]], but was denied after she almost got into a battle with [[Ursa Major (comics)|Ursa Major]].<ref>''Captain America'' #352</ref>

She agreed to join Superia's [[Femizons]] afterward, becoming a supervillain once more. She was attacked by [[Captain America]] and [[Paladin (comics)|Paladin]], whom Gladiatrix manages to capture. The heroes eventually rescued themselves and defeated Gladiatrix afterwards.<ref>''Captain America'' #387-392</ref> She would later be seen attending the  [[Advanced Idea Mechanics|A.I.M.]] Weapons Expo on an island protected by international law.<ref>''Captain America'' #411-414</ref>

===Civil War===
{{Main|Civil War (comics)}}
However, during the Civil War event, Gladiatrix chose the side of the heroes once more and became involved with a gang that was strongly against the Super-Hero Registration Act. Her allies include, but are not limited to, [[Solo (Marvel Comics)|Solo]], [[Typeface (comics)|Typeface]] and [[Battlestar (comics)|Battlestar]]. She is apprehended when a [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]] squad enters their hideout. She almost escapes, being responsible for the visiting journalist [[Sally Floyd (comics)|Sally Floyd]]. Debris knocks her down and Sally escapes on her own.<ref>''Civil War: Front Line'' #4</ref>  She is later seen in the [[Negative Zone]] Prison, being contained in some virtual reality apparatus, begging for someone to help her.<ref>''Amazing Spider-Man'' #535</ref> She, along with the other captive heroes were freed by [[Hulkling]].<ref>''Civil War'' #6</ref> The people he freed assisted Captain America in their fight against the Pro-Registration forces. She did not accept the offer of amnesty.<ref name="civilwarbattledamage">{{Comic book reference | Writer = [[Anthony Flamini]] & [[Ronald Byrd]] | Penciller =  [[Scott Kolins]] | Inker = [[Scott Kolins]] | Story =  | Title = Civil War: Battle Damage Report | Issue = 1 | Date = March 2007 | Publisher = [[Marvel Comics]] | }}</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Gladiatrix has a degree of super-human strength, durability and stamina. She is a skilled wrestler and formidable in hand-to-hand combat.
