'''Nathaniel Richards''' is a fictional [[Time travel|time-traveling]] scientist appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. He is the father of [[superhero]] and leader of the [[Fantastic Four]], [[Mister Fantastic|Reed Richards]]. Though there have been many versions of the character, either as the futuristic villain [[Kang the Conqueror]] or the young superhero [[Iron Lad]], the original Nathaniel is more of an adventurer who has a genuine love for his son.

==Publication history==
Nathaniel Richards first appeared in ''[[Fantastic Four (comic book)|Fantastic Four]]'' #272 and was created by [[John Byrne (comics)|John Byrne]].

==Fictional character biography==
Dr. Nathaniel Richards was a successful scientist who specialized in multiple fields. He was married to [[Evelyn Richards|Evelyn]] and together had a son named Reed who also possessed an intellectual mind like his father. When Evelyn died, Nathaniel continued to raise his son right by supporting his scientific endeavors.<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #570</ref> Later, Nathaniel is approached by the [[S.H.I.E.L.D.#Titles|Brotherhood of the Shield]] and joins their group. Along with [[Howard Stark]], the two meet a super powered individual named Leonid and help him resolve a dispute between the time traveling [[Leonardo da Vinci]] and [[Isaac Newton]] who had split the organization in two. These events somehow resulted in Nathaniel gaining the ability to travel through time.<ref>''S.H.I.E.L.D.'' #1-6</ref><ref>''S.H.I.E.L.D.'' Vol. 2 #1-4</ref>

Nathaniel found himself in the far future along with several other alternate versions of himself. The villain [[Immortus]] had gathered them together and forced them to kill each other off.<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #581</ref> Nathaniel managed to flee and in the process left his family for good, but not before setting his son Reed for life.<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #271-272</ref>

Nathaniel ended up in another dimension where the Earth had become a desolate wasteland when the moon was destroyed. He married a woman named Cassandra who bore two children for him, one of which grew up to be [[Huntara]]. Cassandra turned out to be a power mad individual using his technology to rule the remaining humans. The [[Fantastic Four]] and [[Wyatt Wingfoot]] arrived in this dimension where they defeated Cassandra and freed Nathaniel. After father and son had a heartfelt reunion, Nathaniel stayed behind to help rebuild.<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #272-273</ref>

However, Nathaniel arrived in the present to kidnap [[Franklin Richards (Fantastic Four)|Franklin Richards]].<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #376</ref> Nathaniel aged Franklin to a teenager in an effort to refrain Franklin from having a child with [[Rachel Summers]]. Their child would eventually grow up to become the villain [[Hyperstorm]]<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #414</ref> Nathaniel would help Franklin and the Fantastic Four battle [[Aron (comics)|Aron the Rogue Watcher]] by rescuing [[Uatu|Uatu the Watcher]].<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #400</ref>

Nathaniel later trained [[Invisible Woman|Susan Storm]] to better master her powers.<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #408</ref> 

Nathaniel returned to help Reed jumpstart the [[Future Foundation]], using the technology he gathered over the years.<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #588</ref> He then helped them battle the [[Kree|Kree army]] and the [[Celestial (comics)|Mad Celestials]].<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #600-604</ref><ref>''FF'' #12-15</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
While Nathaniel Richards has no superhuman powers, he does have scientific genius intellect and is also a skilled inventor in advanced machinery and devices.

Nathaniel Richards invented a device called the Time Platform and has grand experience as a time traveler. He also created his own armor which gives him super strength and durability. Nathaniel Richards also carries teleportation devices and energy weapons for defense.

==Other versions==
===What If?===
* In "What if the Fantastic Four were Cosmonauts?", Nathaniel named his son Rudion and was interned in a Siberian Camp after America lost faith in the USSR.<ref>''What If: Fantastic Four'' #1</ref>
* In another reality based on [[Camelot]], Nathaniel Richards is a variation of [[Merlin (Marvel Comics)|Merlin]] to a version of [[Black Knight (comics)|Black Knight]].<ref>''What If?'' Vol. 2 #97</ref>

===The Beast===
A version of Nathaniel Richards exist that was pulled into the far future with the other Nathaniels by [[Immortus]]. This version of Nathaniel, called the Beast, was a homicidal maniac who took the opportunity to slaughter all of his alternate counterparts. The Nathaniel of Earth 616 teams up with young college aged Reed Richards, Ben Grimm and Victor von Doom in order to defeat him.<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #581-582</ref>

===Ultimate Marvel===
In the [[Ultimate Marvel]] Universe, Reed's father is renamed '''Gary Richards''' and he does not have a scientific background. He also seems to despise his son's scientific mind, preferring his friend Ben Grimm's athletic abilities. When the [[Baxter Building]] think tank accepted Reed, Gary was glad that he was gone.<ref>''Ultimate Fantastic Four'' #1</ref> When [[Diablo (Marvel Comics)|Diablo]] kidnapped Reed's younger sister Enid, Gary went to Reed for help though he still resented him.<ref>''Ultimate Fantastic Four'' #42</ref> When Reed quit the Fantastic Four, Gary was there to greet him, not acting surprised.<ref>''Ultimatum Fantastic Four: Requiem''</ref> Gary, along with his wife Mary and two daughters, were killed in an explosion directed at the Richards household.<ref>''Ultimate Enemy'' #1</ref>
