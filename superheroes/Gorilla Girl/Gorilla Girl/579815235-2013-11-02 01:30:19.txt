'''Fahnbullah Eddy''', also known as '''Gorilla Girl''', is a [[fictional character|fictional]] [[comic book]] character within [[Marvel Comics]]' [[shared universe]] the [[Marvel Universe]]. She first appeared in ''[[Marvel Team-Up]]'' #91 (March 1980), and was created by [[Steven Grant]] and [[Pat Broderick]].

==Fictional character biography==
Fahnbullah Eddy,<ref>''Civil War: Battle Damage Report''</ref> originally known as '''Gorilla Woman''', worked at a carnival as a member of the '''Freaks''', a group of [[circus freaks]] that included Muck Monster and Six. When the Freaks fell under the control of the sorcerer [[Moondark]], he forced them to battle [[Spider-Man]].<ref>''Marvel Team-Up'' #91</ref><ref name=Freaks>{{Marvunapp|http://www.marvunapp.com/Appendix/freaksmtu.htm|Freaks}}</ref> Gorilla Girl and the Freaks were later seen in a fight against the criminals [[Hammer and Anvil (comics)|Hammer and Anvil]].<ref name=Freaks/><ref>''Marvel Tales'' #256</ref>

After the events of the [[Civil War (comics)|Civil War]], Gorilla Girl was captured by the [[Thunderbolts (comics)|Thunderbolts]]. During her capture, she threw [[Robbie Baldwin|Penance]] down three flights of stairs, after which Penance beat her severely.<ref>''Thunderbolts: Desperate Measures''</ref> She later [[Superhuman Registration Act|registered]] with the [[Avengers: The Initiative|Initiative]] and joined [[Camp Hammond (comics)|Camp Hammond]] along with [[Annex (comics)|Annex]], [[Prodigy (Ritchie Gilmore)|Prodigy]] and others.<ref>''Avengers: The Initiative'' #13</ref><ref>[http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=preview&id=284 Preview: Avengers:The Initiative #13]</ref>

During the [[Secret Invasion|Skrull invasion]] she is one of the Initiative members to go into battle in [[Times Square]]. She loses teammate Proton; he is executed by the Skrulls. They join the 'Young Avengers' and are saved by the 'Secret Commandos'.<ref>[[Brian Michael Bendis]] (w), [[Leinil Francis Yu]] (p), Mark Morales (i), ''Secret Invasion'' #5-6 (Octoberâ€“November 2008)</ref> Afterwards, Gorilla Girl asked to be put in the Initiative reserves. However, before she gets very far, the [[Thor (Marvel Comics)|Thor]] clone attacks Camp Hammond, and Gorilla Girl fights him before [[Counter Force (Marvel Comics)|Counter Force]] intervenes.<ref>[[Dan Slott]] (w), [[Humberto Ramos]] (a), ''Avengers: The Initiative'' #21 (March 2009)</ref>

Gorilla Girl strikes up a friendship with the alternate universe simian version of [[Speedball (comics)|Speedball]], another Initiative recruit. She becomes involved in the plan of [[Norman Osborn]] to exploit the resources of her friend's home dimension. The two also work with [[Gibbon (comics)|Gibbon]], another simian superhuman.<ref>[[Karl Kesel]] (w), Reilly Brown (p), "This Man... This Monkey!", ''Marvel Apes: Grunt Line Special'' (July, 2009)</ref><ref>[http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=preview&id=2659 Preview: Marvel Apes: Grunt Line Special]</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Fahnbullah Eddy is able to shapeshift into a super-strong, and highly agile [[gorilla]].
