'''Burner''' ('''Byron Calley'''), also known as '''Crucible''' is a fictional [[Mutant (Marvel comics)|mutant]] character in the [[Marvel Comics Universe]] whose first appearance was in ''Captain America Annual'' #4.

==Publication history== 
The fictional character '''Burner''' first appeared in ''Captain America Annual'' #4 in 1977, and was created by [[Jack Kirby]]. Burner subsequently appeared in ''Defenders'' vol. 1 #78-80, 83, 87, 125-126, and 128-130.  As '''Crucible''', he appeared in ''Captain America'' vol. 1 #343, 346, 368, 394, and 426. He appeared once again as Burner in ''New Warriors'' vol. 2, #6.

Burner appeared as part of the "Mutant Force" entry in the ''[[Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe|Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe Deluxe Edition]]'' #9.

==Fictional character biography ==
'''Byron Calley''' was born in [[Secaucus, New Jersey]]. He became a professional criminal, as '''Burner''', and was recruited by [[Magneto (comics)|Magneto]] into his second [[Brotherhood of Mutants|Brotherhood of Evil Mutants]] team. He battled [[Captain America]] in his first mission with the Brotherhood.<ref>''Captain America Annual'' #4</ref> Next, the team renamed itself [[Resistants (comics)|Mutant Force]] when Magneto abandoned them. Mutant Force was employed by the [[Mandrill (comics)|Mandrill]] in his scheme to take over the U.S. Mutant Force battled the [[Valkyrie (Marvel Comics)|Valkyrie]], [[Wasp (comics)|Wasp]], [[Patsy Walker|Hellcat]], [[Nighthawk (Marvel Comics)#Nighthawk (Earth 616 - Kyle Richmond)|Nighthawk]], and [[Henry Pym|Yellowjacket]].<ref>''Defenders'' #78-80</ref> Mutant Force was then recruited into government service, and battled the [[Hulk (comics)|Hulk]].<ref>''Defenders'' #83</ref> Burner testified against the [[Defenders (comics)|Defenders]] before a special government tribunal.<ref>''Defenders'' #87</ref> Mutant Force and [[Mad Dog (comics)|Mad Dog]] were then employed by [[Professor Power]]'s [[Secret Empire]], and battled the Defenders to thwart their attempt to stop the launch of a missile to instigate World War III.<ref>''Defenders'' #129-130</ref>

Some time later, Mutant Force became the ''Resistants'', a mutant terrorist group in opposition to the [[Registration acts (comics)#Mutant Registration Act|Mutant Registration Act]], and mutant rights activists. Burner changed his name to '''Crucible''' during his time with the Resistants. The Reistants rescued the mutant [[Quill (comics)#Quill (Resistants)|Quill]] from the custody of then-current fifth [[U.S. Agent|Captain America]] and [[Battlestar (comics)|Bucky]].<ref>''Captain America'' #343</ref> The Resistants then rescued [[Mentallo]] from the [[Guardsman (comics)|Guardsmen]]'s custody, and battled Captain America and Bucky again. During the battle, Crucible was accidentally non-fatally shot by Mentallo (who had taken the name '''Think-Tank''').<ref>''Captain America'' #346</ref> Crucible was later seen among the Resistants protesting in Washington for mutant rights, where he encountered the original [[Captain America]].<ref>''Captain America'' #368</ref>

He was later seen, again as Burner and part of Mutant Force, fighting against the [[New Warriors]] but was defeated by the young heroes.<ref>''[[New Warriors]]'' vol. 2, #6</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Burner is a [[Mutant (Marvel Comics)|mutant]] that can psionically induce and retard combustion. He can cause something to burst on fire at a maximum distance of approximately 200 feet. He can project flames from his hands like a flamethrower. He can douse a fire at a maximum distance of approximately 200 feet. His mind automatically douses any flame that comes within one foot of him, effectively making him immune to the effects of his own power as well as any fire.

==Other versions==
===Civil War: [[House of M]]===
Burner was shown as a member of Magneto's mutant army during his rise to power.<ref>Civil War: House of M #4</ref>

==Other media==
===Television===
* Burner made a guest appearance in the ''[[X-Men: The Animated Series|X-Men]]'' animated series from the 1990s, though as a member of the [[Acolytes (comics)|Acolytes]], and using his real name: Byron Calley. In the two-part episode ''Sanctuary'', Calley was one of the many mutants that Magneto's offer to live in [[Asteroid M]]. Calley was in charge of overseeing the defensive missiles Magneto had gathered to prevent an attack on the Asteroid. When [[Fabian Cortez]] attempted to fire the missiles to destroy Earth, Calley opposed him. It was also revealed that he was an old friend from [[Gambit (comics)|Gambit]].{{Fact|date=December 2008}}
