The '''Masked Raider''' is an [[American comic book]] character who appeared in [[American comic book]]s published during the 1930s and 1940s period known as the [[Golden Age of Comic Books]]. Created by writer-artist [[Al Anders]], he [[first appeared]] in the [[Timely Comics]]' [[anthology]] series ''[[Marvel Mystery Comics|Marvel Comics]]'' #1 (Oct. 1939), and ran through issue #12 (Oct. 1940) of the by-then retitled ''Marvel Mystery Comics''.

The first [[Western fiction|Western]] character published by Timely, the predecessor of [[Marvel Comics]], the Masked Raider is Jim Gardley, who with his horse Lightning dedicates his life to fighting the lawless and bringing justice to the oppressed.

He is unrelated to the [[Charlton Comics]] series ''[[Billy the Kid (Charlton Comics)|Billy the Kid]]'', which for its first eight issues was titled ''Masked Raider''.
