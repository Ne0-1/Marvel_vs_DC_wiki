'''Sylvester "Snake" Marston''' is a fictional character in the [[Marvel Comics Universe]].

==Publication history==
Snake Marston first appeared in ''[[Machine Man]]'' #16 (August 1980), and was created by [[Tom DeFalco]] and [[Steve Ditko]].

The character subsequently appeared in ''Marvel Team-Up'' #138 (February 1984), ''Thunderbolts'' #104 (September 2006), and #107 (December 2006).

Snake Marston appeared as part of the "Enforcers" entry in the ''[[Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe|Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe Deluxe Edition]]'' #4.

==Fictional character biography==
Sylvester Marston was born in [[Ontario, Canada]].  He was a member of [[Baron Brimstone]]'s [[Satan Squad]] when they fought [[Machine Man]].{{issue|date=December 2012}} He later joined the [[Enforcers (comics)|Enforcers]] and fought [[Spider-Man]].{{issue|date=December 2012}} He was impersonated by the [[Eel (comics)|Eel]] as part of a plot involving [[Daredevil (Marvel Comics character)|Daredevil]], [[Mister Hyde (comics)|Mister Hyde]], and the Enforcers.<ref>''Daredevil'' #356-357</ref>

Much later, he was seen imprisoned in Seagate Prison's supervillain cell block.{{issue|date=March 2013}}

===Civil War===
During the ''[[Civil War (comics)|Civil War]]'' event, he was captured by [[Baron Zemo]]'s [[Thunderbolts (comics)|Thunderbolts]] and forced to join them or face jail time.{{Issue|date=April 2009}} With the Thunderbolts army, he went on a mission to Sydney, Australia.{{issue|date=March 2013}}

==Powers and abilities==
Snake Marston has no super human powers; he is however a master contortionist who can hold an opponent helpless by winding himself around them.

==In other media==
===Video games===
* Snake Marston is mentioned in ''[[Spider-Man: Shattered Dimensions]]''. [[Hammerhead (comics)|Hammerhead]]'s henchmen mentioned how Marston uses his contortionist abilities to attempt to seduce [[Frederick Foswell|Janice Foswell]], something her boyfriend Hammerhead is unhappy about.
