'''Skull the Slayer''' ('''James Patrick Scully''') is a fictional [[Marvel Comics]] character that debuted in his own book in August, 1975.

==Publication history==
Skull the Slayer had his own ongoing series starting in August 1975, which ran for eight issues.

==Fictional character biography==
Skull the Slayer is a trained soldier turned superhero who wears a Scorpion power belt that enhances his strength and durability. The belt also has preservative effects on his body's metabolism. On one occasion, Scully was able to funnel energy into explosive force.{{Issue|date=April 2009}} The full capabilities of the belt are unknown.

Jim Scully was an adventurer whose plane went through a time warp in the [[Bermuda Triangle]], marooning him and three companions in an alternate Earth where dinosaurs, primitives, and aliens co-existed. Scully and his three companions were eventually rescued and returned to their own world by the [[Thing (comics)|Thing]] of the [[Fantastic Four]].<ref>''Marvel Two-in-One'' #35-36</ref> He served in [[Doctor Druid]]'s team of occult investigators the [[Shock Troop (comics)|Shock Troop]] alongside [[Sepulchre (comics)|Sepulchre]] and [[N'Kantu, the Living Mummy]].<ref>''Quasar'' #45-46</ref> He was attempting to find a way to remove the power belt which had recently been damaged and had altered his appearance. His skin had become transparent showing only a green glowing skelton. During this time, he had adopted the alias of the obscure Golden Age hero, the [[Blazing Skull]].<ref>''Captain America'' #420</ref> He crossed paths with heroes such as [[Quasar (comics)|Quasar]] and [[Captain America]], while a member of the team. Eventually, Doctor Druid was able to restore him to his normal appearance and he left the team.{{Issue|date=August 2010}} Sometime later, attempts to remove the belt caused him lose the youthful metabolism the belt provided.<ref>''Hawkeye'' v3 #1</ref> He gained weight and lost much of the physical shape the belt had kept him at over the years.{{Issue|date=August 2010}} While serving as a bodyguard, he met and had an adventure with [[Hawkeye (comics)|Hawkeye]].{{Issue|date=August 2010}}

===There and Back Again===
Skull the Slayer found his way back to the alternate earth as the member of a prospecting expedition, along with [[Lee Forrester]], and sent a message back to our earth asking to be saved.{{issue|date=August 2014}} The [[Future Foundation]] found the message buoy and contacted the [[X-Men]] (since Forrester, who had recorded the message, had specifically asked for [[Cyclops (comics)|Cyclops]] and [[Magneto (comics)|Magneto]]), and the two teams travelled through the portal to the other world.<ref>''X-Men'' #16</ref> The heroes discovered that time passes differently in the new realm and the survivors had experienced three years passing since the buoy had been launched.{{issue|date=August 2014}} After an invasion attempt by the alien Emperor against both groups' native Earth was thwarted by the efforts of the heroes, Skull and Lee, who had fallen in love with each other, decided to stay behind and safeguard two artifacts which would ensure peace between the alternate Earth's tribes.<ref>''X-Men'' #19</ref>
