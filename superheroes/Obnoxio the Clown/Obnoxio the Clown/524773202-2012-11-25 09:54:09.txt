'''Obnoxio the Clown''' was the [[mascot]] of the [[Marvel Comics]] humor magazine ''[[Crazy (magazine)|Crazy]]''.<ref name="obnoxio">[http://daveslongbox.blogspot.com/2005/04/obnoxio-clown-marvel-comics-1983.html Obnoxio the Clown] information, Accessed August 2008.</ref> 

== Character ==
Obnoxio was portrayed as a slovenly, vulgar, cigar-puffing middle-aged man in a torn and dirty [[clown suit]], with a dyspeptic and [[Cynicism (contemporary)|cynical]] attitude.

== Comic book ==
'''''Obnoxio the Clown''''' was also the title of a one-shot [[Marvel Comics|Marvel]] [[comic book]] that was published in April 1983.<ref name="obnoxio" /> The plot centered on Obnoxio the Clown as a villain and unlikely ally against the [[X-Men]].<ref name="obnoxio" /> He and the group, at the [[X-Mansion]], 'team-up' against Eye Scream, a villain that can transform into various types of ice cream. Only one issue was ever produced.

The sole issue of the comic book was written, illustrated, colored, and lettered by [[Alan Kupperberg]];<ref>Comic Collector live. [http://www.comiccollectorlive.com/LiveData/Issue.aspx?id=acf47b1c-3668-4a8b-952f-9de5123de7c4 Obnoxio The Clown #1.] Accessed November 2008.</ref> in a comment which Kupperberg attributes to [[Peter David]],<ref>[http://www.alankupperberg.com/comicbook.html Kupperberg interview] from ''[[Comic Book Artist]]'' #16, archived at AlanKupperberg.com</ref> and which David attributes to [[James Owsley]],<ref>[http://www.peterdavid.net/index.php/2010/05/07/not-in-any-store/ Not in Any Store…], by Peter David, from [[Comics Buyer's Guide]] #1003, February 5, 1993; archived at PeterDavid.net</ref> the comic was described as "Written, penciled, inked and lettered by Alan Kupperberg. Yes, it’s untouched by human hands."

== Novel ==
In the alternate reality depicted in the "Doctor Doom: The Chaos Trilogy" novels, written by Steven A. Roman, Obnoxio has a popular slapstick comedy show. Like anything on television, the show is approved by Doctor Doom's administration.{{Citation needed|date=April 2010}}
