'''Turk Barrett''' is a [[fictional character]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].

He has had many run-ins with [[Daredevil (Marvel Comics character)|Daredevil]] where his inept schemes are usually played as [[comic relief]].<ref name="comicbookdb1"/>

==Publication history==
{{expand section|date=August 2015}}

Turk Barrett first appears in ''[[Daredevil (Marvel Comics series)|Daredevil]]'' (vol. 1) #69 (October 1970) and was created by writers [[Gary Friedrich]] and [[Roy Thomas]] and artist [[Gene Colan]].<ref name="comicbookdb1">{{cite web|url=http://www.comicbookdb.com/character.php?ID=8652 |title=Turk Barrett |publisher=Comic Book DB |date= |accessdate=2010-06-08}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marvunapp.com/Appendix/turkbarretdd.htm |title=Turk (Daredevil character) |publisher=Marvunapp.com |date= |accessdate=2010-06-08}}</ref>

==Fictional character biography==
{{expand section|date=May 2015}}
Turk Barrett was a small-time crook operating in Hell's Kitchen. [[Fixer (comics)|Roscoe Sweeney]] once sent Barrett to pay [[Jack Murdock (comics)|Jack Murdock]] to throw a boxing match.<ref>''Daredevil: Battlin' Jack Murdock'' #1</ref> Barrett was associated with a street gang called the Thunderbolts.<ref>''Daredevil'' #69</ref>

Barrett also worked for Eric Slaughter.<ref>''Daredevil'' #159</ref> Barrett once stole the [[Mauler (comics)|Mauler]] armor to confront [[Daredevil (Marvel Comics character)|Daredevil]]. Barrett, however, is defeated in seconds.<ref>''Daredevil'' #176 (Nov. 1981)</ref> Turk later stole the [[Stilt-Man]] armor. He offered his services to the [[Kingpin (comics)|Kingpin]], but was declined. The Kingpin contended to not employ "idiots". Wilbur Day contacted Daredevil with knowledge of how to defeat Barrett.<ref>''Daredevil'' #179. Marvel Comics.</ref>

Turk has spent quite a bit of time at Josie's Bar in New York City. His primary associate is "Grotto" who often reluctantly participates in Turk's schemes. Turk once mugged a "Santa" at Christmas and intended to use the Yuletide attire to swindel charitable donations from others. In this instance when he was confronted by Matt Murdock, Turk stabbed the disoriented Murdock seriously, almost fatally, wounding Matt.{{issue|date=March 2015}}

During the 2016 "[[Civil War II]]" storyline, Turk Barrett is with Kingpin when they hear from a barista named Armand that his girlfriend named Sonia went missing. They track her down to a human trafficking business that [[Man Mountain Marko]] and Kingpin's former minion Janus Jardeesh were working for.<ref>''Civil War II: Kingpin'' #1. Marvel Comics.</ref>

==Other versions==
In the alternate timeline of the 2005 storyline "[[House of M]]", Turk Barrett is a member of [[Diamondback (Willis Stryker)|Willis Stryker]]'s gang. After Stryker was defeated, Turk willingly followed [[Luke Cage]] when he took control of the group.<ref>''House of M: Avengers'' #1. Marvel Comics.</ref>

The [[Ultimate Marvel]] version of Turk Barrett is a gangster. A criminal acquaintance of [[Prowler (comics)|Aaron Davis]], he has [[Jefferson Davis (comics)|Jefferson Davis]] as his main enforcer, eventually leaving his territory to Wilson Fisk's criminal empire.<ref>''Miles Morales: Ultimate Spider-Man'' #8</ref>

During the ''[[Secret Wars (2015 comic book)|Secret Wars]]'' storyline, a variation of Turk Barrett resides in the [[Battleworld]] domain of the Valley of Doom which is based from the remnants of Earth-51920 where every Marvel character is depicted in [[American frontier|Wild West]] form. He is one of the minions of Mayor Wilson Fisk. Barrett and the rest of Mayor Fisk's men attack [[Red Wolf (comics)|Red Wolf]] when he tried to destroy [[Roxxon Energy Corporation|Roxxon]] Dam. Upon being tipped off by [[Ben Urich]], Sheriff [[Captain America|Steve Rogers]] intervened and was able to put him in sheriff custody. Later that night, Mayor Fisk sent Turk and his men to lure Sheriff Rogers out of the Sheriff Department so that they can kill Red Wolf. This plan didn't go well as Sheriff Rogers and Red Wolf were able to kill Turk and those involved in the attempt on Red Wolf's life.<ref>''1872'' #1</ref>

Later in the episode, [[Luke Cage]] tracks Turk down and traps him in a dumpster after scaring him into giving up Diamondback's warehouse.<ref>{{cite episode|title=Soliloquy of Chaos|series=Marvel's Luke Cage|credits=Abraham, Phil (director); Akela Cooper & Charles Murray (writer)|network=[[Netflix]]|airdate=September 30, 2016|season=1|number=12}}</ref>
** Turk makes another appearance in ''[[The Defenders (miniseries)|The Defenders]]''. In addition, he also told Luke that Mariah Dillard and Shades have "ghosted."
