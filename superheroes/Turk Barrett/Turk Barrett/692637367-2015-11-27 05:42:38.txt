'''Turk Barrett''' is a [[fictional character]] that appears in comic books published by [[Marvel Comics]].

Turk has had many run-ins with [[Daredevil (Marvel Comics)|Daredevil]] where his inept schemes are usually played as [[comic relief]].<ref name="comicbookdb1"/>

==Publication history==
{{expand section|date=August 2015}}

Turk Barrett first appears in ''[[Daredevil (Marvel Comics)|Daredevil]]'' (vol. 1) #69 (October 1970) and was created by writers [[Gary Friedrich]] and [[Roy Thomas]] and artist [[Gene Colan]].<ref name="comicbookdb1">{{cite web|url=http://www.comicbookdb.com/character.php?ID=8652 |title=Turk Barrett |publisher=Comic Book DB |date= |accessdate=2010-06-08}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marvunapp.com/Appendix/turkbarretdd.htm |title=Turk (Daredevil character) |publisher=Marvunapp.com |date= |accessdate=2010-06-08}}</ref>

==Fictional character biography==
{{expand section|date=May 2015}}
Turk Barrett was a small-time crook operating in Hell's Kitchen. [[Roscoe Sweeney]] once sent Turk Barrett to pay Jack Murdock to throw a boxing match.<ref>''Daredevil: Battlin' Jack Murdock'' #1</ref> Barrett was associated with a street gang called the Thunderbolts.{{issue|date=May 2015}}

Barrett worked for [[Eric Slaughter]].{{issue|date=May 2015}} Barrett once stole the Mauler armor to confront Daredevil. Barrett, however, is defeated in seconds.<ref>''Daredevil'' #176 (Nov. 1981)</ref> Turk later stole the [[Stilt-Man|Stilt-Man armor]]. He offered his services to the [[Kingpin (comics)|Kingpin]] but was declined. The Kingpin contended that he did not employ "idiots". The original creator of the Stilt-Man armor, Wilbur Day, contacted Daredevil with knowledge of how to defeat Barrett.<ref>''Daredevil'' #179</ref>

Turk has spent quite a bit of time at Josie's Bar in New York City. His primary associate is "Grotto" who often reluctantly participates in Turk's schemes. Turk once mugged a "Santa" at Christmas and intended to use the Yuletide attire to swindel charitable donations from others. In this instance when he was confronted by a disoriented Matt Murdock, Turk stabbed Murdock seriously, almost fatally, wounding him.{{issue|date=March 2015}}

==Other versions==
In the ''[[House of M]]'' reality, Turk was a member of [[Diamondback (comics)|Willis Stryker]]'s gang. After Stryker was defeated, Turk willingly followed [[Luke Cage]] when he took control of the group.<ref>''House of M: Avengers'' #1</ref>

==In other media==

===Television===
* Turk Barrett appears in ''[[Daredevil (TV series)|Marvel's Daredevil]]'' series on Netflix, portrayed by Rob Morgan.<ref>{{cite web| first= Marc |last= Strom|title='Marvel's Daredevil' For Netflix Reveals 6 Supporting Cast Members|url=http://marvel.com/news/tv/24243/marvels_daredevil_for_netflix_reveals_6_supporting_cast_members|website=Marvel.com|publisher=Marvel|accessdate=12 April 2015|date=11 Mar 2015|quote=[...]Rob Morgan (“Person of Interest,” “Believe”) will play Turk Barrett, a smalltime criminal in Hell’s Kitchen[...]}}</ref> In this series, Barrett is a mob enforcer and arms seller who has a love of semi-automatic pistols and takes personal pleasure in criminal activities. In the episode "World on Fire," [[Kingpin (comics)|Wilson Fisk]] pays Turk Barrett to reveal to Vladimir Ranskahov that his brother Anatoly was killed by Fisk. At the end of season 1 during the episode "Daredevil," Barrett is among those who are arrested by the FBI for his association with Wilson Fisk's empire.

===Film===
* Turk Barrett appears in the TV film ''[[The Trial of the Incredible Hulk]]'', played by [[Mark Acheson]].

* Turk was mentioned in the Director's cut of ''[[Daredevil (film)|Daredevil]]'' by the character Dante Jackson (played by [[Coolio]]).
