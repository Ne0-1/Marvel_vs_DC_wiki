'''Libra''' ('''Gustav Brandt''') is a [[Marvel Comics]] [[Character (arts)|character]], a former [[supervillain]] in the [[Marvel Comics Universe]], most notably a member of the [[Zodiac (comics)|Zodiac Cartel]].

==Publication history==
The original Libra first appeared in ''[[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]]'' #72 (January 1970), and was created by [[Roy Thomas]] and [[Sal Buscema]].

The character subsequently appears in:
* ''Avengers'' #80-82 (September-November 1970),
* ''Daredevil'' #69 (October 1970),
* ''Iron Man'' #33-36 (January-April 1971),
* ''Daredevil'' #73 (February 1971),
* ''Avengers'' #120-125 (February-July 1974), #130-134 (December 1974-April 1975),
* ''Giant-Size Avengers'' #3 (February 1975),
* ''Avengers'' #135 (May 1975),
* ''Giant-Size Avengers'' #4 (June 1975),
* ''Iron Man'' #184-185 (July-August 1984),
* ''West Coast Avengers'' #26 (November 1987),
* ''[[Avengers Forever]]'' #1-12 (December 1998-February 2000).

Libra appeared as part of the "Zodiac" entry in the ''[[Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe|Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe Deluxe Edition]]'' #20.

==Fictional character biography==
===Gustav Brandt===
[[Germany|German]] soldier Gustav Brandt served with the [[France|French]] forces during the [[Vietnam War]].  While in [[Saigon]], he met and fell in love with a [[Vietnamese people|Vietnamese]] woman named Lua, and eventually had a daughter with her.  However, Lua's brother, Khruul, was enraged with the [[interracial couple|interracial coupling]] of his sister and a [[European ethnic groups|European]] man.  He and his men attacked Gustav and his family with flamethrowers.  Lua was killed in the ensuing battle, and Gustav lost his eyesight.  He and his daughter took shelter in [[Pama]], where he was trained by the priests, but was forbidden to communicate with his daughter.

Joining the [[Zodiac (comics)|Zodiac Cartel]], Gustav adopted the codename Libra, and served as a terrorist. The Zodiac was infiltrated by Nick Fury, posing as Scorpio; the Zodiac fought the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]] and escaped.<ref>''Avengers'' #72</ref> Led by Taurus, the Zodiac later attempted to kill all Manhattan residents born under the sign of Gemini as a show of power, but were thwarted by the Avengers. Taurus's faction attempted to kill the Zodiac dissident faction, but all twelve leaders were captured by the Avengers.<ref>''Avengers'' #120-122</ref> Battling the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]] on several occasions, he eventually discovered that [[Mantis (Marvel Comics)|Mantis]] was the daughter he had left in Pama, and turned himself in to the authorities.{{Issue|date=July 2009}} He then vanished mysteriously, later turning up in [[Ho Chi Minh City]], where the [[Swordsman (comics)|Swordsman]] revealed to everyone Mantis' transformation into the Celestial Madonna.{{Issue|date=July 2009}} Libra then witnessed the marriage of Mantis and the Swordsman, and returned to prison shortly after.{{Issue|date=July 2009}}  He did not stay in prison long, though, as he escaped and committed illegal acts alongside the Zodiac once more.{{Issue|date=July 2009}} A new android version of the Zodiac later appeared, led by Scorpio in a new android body, massacred the human Zodiac, and took over their criminal operations.<ref>''West Coast Avengers'' Vol. 2 #26</ref>

After the Zodiac's death at the hands of a [[Life Model Decoy]], Libra served alongside the Avengers briefly as they battled [[Kang (comics)|Kang]] and [[Immortus]], his ability to sense the 'balance' of the universe helping [[Rick Jones (comics)|Rick Jones]] use the [[Destiny Force]] to summon seven Avengers from different points in the team's history who would be crucial to their victory in the current crisis, each Avenger selected at this particular moment in time thanks to Libra's subconscious awareness of the universal balance and the necessity of their predicted actions<ref>''Avengers Forever'' #1-#12</ref>. He is presumably residing in The Between, a mystical world.{{Issue|date=July 2009}}

===Android Zodiac===
Scorpio (Jake Fury) constructed the android Zodiac members, although his plan was thwarted by the [[Defenders (comics)|Defenders]].<ref>''Defenders'' #48-50</ref> The androids Libra and Sagittarius employed costumed criminals posing as the Defenders to create chaos in New York City.<ref>''Defenders'' #63-65</ref> [[Quicksilver (comics)|Quicksilver]] employed the android Zodiac to attack the [[West Coast Avengers]], but the Avengers defeated the Zodiac.<ref>''West Coast Avengers Annual'' #1</ref>

Led by Scorpio in a new android body, the android Zodiac massacred the human Zodiac, and took over their criminal operations.  They battled the West Coast Avengers, but were rendered inert when they were transported to the dimension of the Brotherhood of the Ankh.<ref>''West Coast Avengers'' Vol. 2 #26-28</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Libra is physically blind, but possesses psychic senses, and is also a skilled martial artist. He has the ability to shift into the Place Between Places, a mystical dimension that is untraceable to most life-forms.

==In other media==
===Television===
* Libra (alongside the other Zodiac members) appears in ''[[The Avengers: United They Stand]]''. In this show, Libra is a female alien who wields a weighing scale-like weapon.
