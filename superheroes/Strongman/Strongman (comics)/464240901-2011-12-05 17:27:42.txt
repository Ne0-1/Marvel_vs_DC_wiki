The '''Strongman''' is a fictional character in the [[Marvel Universe]].

==Publication history==
The Strongman first appeared in ''[[Hulk (comics)|Incredible Hulk]]'' #3 (September 1962), and was created by [[Stan Lee]] and [[Steve Ditko]].

The character subsequently appears in ''The Amazing Spider-Man'' #16 (September 1964), ''The Avengers'' #22 (November 1965), ''Thor'' #145 (October 1967), ''Ghost Rider'' #72-73 (September-October 1982), ''The Sensational She-Hulk'' #1 (May 1989), and ''X-Men and Power Pack'' #3 (February 2006).

The Strongman appeared as part of the "Circus of Crime" entry in the ''[[Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe|Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe Deluxe Edition]]'' #2.

==Fictional character biography==
Bruce Olafsen was born in [[Stockholm, Sweden]], but later came to the U.S. and became a naturalized American citizen.  As a long time member of the criminal organization, the [[Circus of Crime]], Bruto the Strongman works as a circus strongman and weight-lifter.  He has particularly strong biceps and teeth.

He was once defeated by [[Hawkeye (comics)|Hawkeye]].{{Issue|date=June 2011}}

==Powers and abilities==
Bruto has no superhuman powers but is strong and a skilled wrestler.

==Other media==
A variation of Strongman appears in the ''[[Spider-Man (1981 TV series)|Spider-Man]]'' episode "Carnival of Crime."

==DC Comics==
Empowered by [[Amos Fortune (comics)|Amos Fortune]] as part of his [[List of Justice League enemies|Luck League]], Strongman drained the powers of [[Superman]] in ''[[Justice League of America]]'' #151 (February 1978).
