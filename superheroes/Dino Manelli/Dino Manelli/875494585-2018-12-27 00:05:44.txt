'''Dino Manelli''' is a [[fictional character]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. His first appearance was in ''[[Sgt. Fury and his Howling Commandos]]'' vol. 1 #1.

==Fictional character biography==
Before the war, '''Dino Manelli''' was a charismatic actor who was fluent in [[German language|German]] and [[Italian language|Italian]]. Manelli was a member of the original Howling Commandos and fought alongside the team during [[World War II]]. He was briefly replaced by [[Eric Koenig]] when he was sent on a special mission, which was helping to organize another team known as the [[Deadly Dozen (comics)|Deadly Dozen]].{{Issue|date=February 2010}} He later returned to the Howlers but was briefly off-duty when he was wounded.{{Issue|date=February 2010}} After the war, he continued acting and when he rejoins the Howlers for a one off assignment during the [[Vietnam War]] he has his own television show (as Dean Martin did at the time).<ref>''Sgt Fury Annual'' #3</ref> He later assisted [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]] following the Deltite Affair.{{Issue|date=February 2010}}

==Other media==

===Television===
* Dino Manelli appears in ''[[The Avengers: Earth's Mightiest Heroes]]'' episode "Meet Captain America." He is seen as a member of the Howling Commandos. His ghost appears to Captain America in Hel when Rogers is stranded there.

===Video games===
* In ''[[Marvel: Ultimate Alliance]]'', the son of Dino Manelli makes a quick cameo. Dino Manelli Jr. (voiced by [[Dave Wittenberg]]) is rescued by the player's team in the Helicarrier and in his journal he speaks of his time joining [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]]. He later appears in the Omega Base level, where he requires the player's team to use an anti-aircraft gun in a helicopter so he can open a door. He also has a special conversation with Deadpool (who insults Dino for being stupid and asks for money in return of rescuing him).
