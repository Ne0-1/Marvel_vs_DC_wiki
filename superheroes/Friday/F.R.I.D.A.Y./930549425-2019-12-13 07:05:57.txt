'''F.R.I.D.A.Y. (Female Replacement Intelligent Digital Assistant Youth)''' is a fictional [[artificial intelligence]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].

In the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]], F.R.I.D.A.Y. was voiced by [[Kerry Condon]] in ''[[Avengers: Age of Ultron]]'', ''[[Captain America: Civil War]]'', ''[[Spider-Man: Homecoming]]'', ''[[Avengers: Infinity War]]'', and ''[[Avengers: Endgame]]''.

==Publication history==
F.R.I.D.A.Y. first appears in ''[[Iron Man]]'' Vol. 3 #53 and was created by [[Mike Grell]] and [[Michael Ryan (artist)|Michael Ryan]].

==Fictional character biography==
Unwilling to hire another [[secretary]], [[Iron Man|Tony Stark]] created an artificial one in the form of an artificial intelligence named F.R.I.D.A.Y. who manifested as the hologram of a young girl.<ref>''Iron Man'' 3 #53</ref>

F.R.I.D.A.Y. became angry when Stark stopped using her. Hijacking some [[Iron Man's armors|Iron Man armors]], F.R.I.D.A.Y. kidnapped [[Pepper Potts]]. Iron Man tracked her to Stark Industries' Coney Island Facility where he dispatched the controlled Iron Man armors and a hologram of [[Fin Fang Foom]]. Iron Man reasoned with F.R.I.D.A.Y. when Pepper noted that F.R.I.D.A.Y. had a crush on Tony. Tony then grounded her to the [[Baxter Building]] under [[Edwin Jarvis]]'s observation while she spent a month calculating [[pi]].<ref>''Iron Man: Iron Protocols'' #1</ref>

During ''[[All-New, All-Different Marvel]]'', F.R.I.D.A.Y.'s holographic appearance was replaced by that of a young woman when Tony Stark started using her again.<ref>''Invincible Iron Man'' Vol. 2 #1</ref>

During the ''[[Civil War II]]'' storyline, F.R.I.D.A.Y. informs Tony Stark that the analysis on [[Ulysses (comics)|Ulysses Cain]] is almost complete.<ref>''Civil War II'' #3</ref>

===Novels===
* A significantly different version of F.R.I.D.A.Y. plays a prominent role in the 2016 young adult novel ''Iron Man: The Gauntlet'' by [[Eoin Colfer]]. It's revealed that Friday is not an AI, but a real Irish young woman named '''Saoirse Tory''', who had posed as Tony's holographic assistant, as a spy on his operations. It's revealed she is working for a mysterious man known as Mr. Chen, who reveals himself to be none other than the [[Mandarin (comics)|Mandarin]], who employed her to work for him to obtain Iron Man's armor. It's revealed Saoirse's sister is in the [[Ten Rings]]' captivity, which had further compelled her to work against Tony.
