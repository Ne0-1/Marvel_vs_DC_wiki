{{Short description|Name of some fictional characters in Marvel Comics}}'''Black Widow''' is the name of several [[fictional]] characters appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. Most of these versions exist in Marvel's main [[shared universe]], known as the [[Marvel Universe]].

==Claire Voyant==
{{main|Black Widow (Claire Voyant)}}
'''Claire Voyant''' is the first costumed, superpowered female protagonist in comic books. Created by writer [[George Kapitan]] and artist [[Harry Sahle]], she first appeared in ''[[Mystic Comics]]'' #4 (Aug. 1940). She kills evildoers to deliver their souls to [[Satan]], her master. The character is unrelated to the later Marvel Comics superheroines who took on the codename.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://screenrant.com/black-widow-body-trivia-facts/|title=20 Weird Things About Black Widow Even Hardcore Fans Might Not Know - 3|last=Gerber|first=Jamie|website=[[Screen Rant]]|date=Jun 27, 2018|access-date=May 29, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190203203421/https://screenrant.com/black-widow-body-trivia-facts/|archive-date=February 3, 2019}}</ref>

==Natasha Romanoff==
{{main|Black Widow (Natasha Romanova)|Natasha Romanoff (Marvel Cinematic Universe)}}
'''Natasha Romanoff'''<ref>{{cite comic|title=[[Wolverine: Origins]]|volume=1|issue=16|writer=[[Daniel Way]]|artist=[[Steve Dillon]]|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]|date=October, 2007}}</ref> is the first character to take on the Black Widow codename in the modern mainstream Marvel Comics. She was created by  editor and plotter [[Stan Lee]], scripter [[Don Rico]] and artist [[Don Heck]], and first appeared in ''[[Tales of Suspense]]'' #52 (April 1964). The character has been associated with several superhero teams in the Marvel Universe, including the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]], the [[Defenders (comics)|Defenders]], the [[Champions (1975 team)|Champions]], [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]], and the [[Thunderbolts (comics)|Thunderbolts]]. She has appeared in many other forms of media, including the major motion pictures ''[[Iron Man 2]]'', ''[[The Avengers (2012 film)|The Avengers]]'', ''[[Captain America: The Winter Soldier]]'', ''[[Avengers: Age of Ultron]]'', ''[[Captain America: Civil War]]'', ''[[Avengers: Infinity War]]'', ''[[Avengers: Endgame]]'' and ''[[Black Widow (2021 film)|Black Widow]]'', wherein she is portrayed by [[Scarlett Johansson]].

==Yelena Belova==
{{main|Black Widow (Yelena Belova)}}

'''Yelena Belova''' is the second character to take on the Black Widow codename in the modern mainstream comics who debuted briefly in ''[[Inhumans]]'' #5 (March 1999) and was fully introduced in the 1999 [[Marvel Knights]] mini-series ''Black Widow''. A second miniseries, also titled ''Black Widow'' and featuring Natasha Romanoff and [[Daredevil (Marvel Comics character)|Daredevil]], followed in 2001. The next year, she did a solo turn in her own three-issue miniseries titled ''Black Widow: Pale Little Spider'' under the mature-audience [[Marvel MAX]] imprint. This June to August 2002 story arc, by writer [[Greg Rucka]] and artist [[Igor Kordey]], was a [[Flashback (narrative)|flashback]] to the story of her being the second modern Black Widow, in events preceding her ''Inhumans'' appearance.<ref name="CBRrevisit">{{cite web|url=https://www.cbr.com/revisiting-marvels-beezer-belova/|title=Revisiting Marvel's Beezer & Belova|last=De Blieck Jr.|first=Augie|publisher=[[Comic Book Resources]]|date= July 16, 2013|access-date=June 22, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190616051339/https://www.cbr.com/revisiting-marvels-beezer-belova/|archive-date=June 16, 2019}}</ref> [[Florence Pugh]] portrays Yelena in the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe|MCU]] film ''Black Widow''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://io9.gizmodo.com/heres-what-happened-in-the-black-widow-footage-marvel-s-1836393723|title=Here's What Happened in the Black Widow Footage Marvel Showed at Comic-Con|last=Lussier|first=Germain|website=[[io9]]|date=July 20, 2019|access-date=June 30, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190721060738/https://io9.gizmodo.com/heres-what-happened-in-the-black-widow-footage-marvel-s-1836393723|archive-date=July 21, 2019}}</ref>

==Alternative universe versions==
===Monica Chang===
{{Further|Monica Chang}}

===Jessica Drew===
{{Further|Spider-Woman (Ultimate Marvel character)}}
The [[Ultimate Marvel|Ultimate]] version of Jessica Drew is a female clone of [[Spider-Man (Ultimate Marvel character)|Spider-Man (Peter Parker)]] that goes by the Black Widow alias.<ref name="allnew1">{{cite comic|title=All-New Ultimates|volume=1|issue=#1|writer=[[Michel Fiffe]]|artist=Amilcar Pinna|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]|date=April 9, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://m.apnews.com/ap/db_268764/contentdetail.htm?contentguid=gPxzbSry|title=In Marvel's Ultimate universe, fate looms large|last=Moore|first=Matt|publisher=[[Associated Press]]|date=January 10, 2014|access-date=May 29, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140110222853/http://m.apnews.com/ap/db_268764/contentdetail.htm?contentguid=gPxzbSry|archive-date=January 10, 2014}}</ref>

===Black Widow 2099===
The futuristic [[Marvel 2099|2099]] version of Black Widow is an African-American woman named '''Tania'''. She operates as part of the Avengers 2099 at the [[Alchemax]] corporation's behest.<ref>{{cite comic|title=Secret Wars 2099|volume=#1|issue=|writer=[[Peter David]]|artist=[[Will Sliney]]|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]|date=May, 2015}}</ref> Like black widow spiders, she literally eats her mates after having sex with them.<ref>{{cite comic|title=Secret Wars 2099|volume=1|issue=#3|writer=[[Peter David]]|artist=[[Will Sliney]]|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]|date=July, 2015}}</ref>

===Dottie Underwood===
The ''[[Agent Carter (TV series)|Agent Carter]]'' television series features [[Dottie Underwood]] (portrayed by [[Bridget Regan]]), a 1946 precursor to Black Widow and an operative of [[Leviathan (Marvel Comics)|Leviathan]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ign.com/articles/2015/01/28/marvels-agent-carter-dottie-black-widow|title=Marvel's Agent Carter Exclusive: Showrunners Reveal Who Dottie Works For|last=Goldman|first=Eric|publisher=IGN|date=January 28, 2015|access-date=May 26, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190406070329/https://www.ign.com/articles/2015/01/28/marvels-agent-carter-dottie-black-widow|archive-date=April 6, 2019}}</ref>
