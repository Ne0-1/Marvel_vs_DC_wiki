The [[Eternal (comics)|Eternals]] are a race of cosmic beings in [[Marvel Comics]].  The following is a list of known Eternals in the Marvel Universe.

== Membership==

===Earth===
*[[Ikaris]]<ref name="The Eternals #1">''The Eternals'' #1</ref> - A Polar Eternal, son of [[Virako]] and [[Tulayn]], father of [[Icarus]]. It is not known what Ikaris' birth-name was; he adopted the name "Ikaris" after his son, the [[Icarus]] of legend, was killed. Became Prime Eternal after challenging [[Thena]]. Under the alias of "Sovereign", introduced the Eternals to the world as a team of super-heroes called the "New Breed"
*[[Ajak]]<ref>''The Eternals'' #2</ref> - Polar Eternal born to Rakar and Amaa, brother of Arex. With Arex, they were known in Greece as Ajax the Greater and Ajax the Lesser. Disintegrated himself after he discovered he had killed many sets of human twins in ''Eternals: The Herod Factor'' while seeking [[Donald & Deborah Ritter]].
*[[Sersi]]<ref>''The Eternals'' #3</ref> - Manipulates molecules and atoms.  Daughter of [[Helios]] and [[Perse]]. Sersi is the only Eternal who is a fifth-level adept at matter manipulation. Became famous in [[Greek Mythology]] through her encounter with [[Odysseus]]. Prefers to live among humans and likes to throw parties. Became [[Avengers (comics)|Avenger]] after [[Captain America]] requested her aid undercover as a teenager. Put herself into exile, but has since returned. Lover of Makkari.
*[[Domo (comics)|Domo]]<ref name="The Eternals #5">''The Eternals'' #5</ref> - Former Head Technologist at the Temple of Command in [[Olympia (comics)|Olympia]]. Domo was extremely dedicated to his work, which involved monitoring all of Olympia's advanced systems. Was responsible for contacting Earth's Eternals when it was time to form the Uni-Mind. Domo was among the Eternals who left Earth in the form of a Uni-Mind.
*[[Makkari (comics)|Makkari]]<ref name="The Eternals #5"/> - Superhumanly fast; Son of Verona and Mara. Speedy and impatient, many Eternals don't take him seriously. In the 1940s, Makkari was the superheroes "Hurricane" and "Mercury."  Later he founded the [[Monster Hunters]] to fight [[Kro]] and joined the [[First Line]] as "Major Mercury". In the 2006 series he becomes the emissary of the [[Dreaming Celestial]].
*[[Thena]]<ref name="The Eternals #5"/> - Daughter of [[Zuras]] and [[Cybele]]. Originally named Azura, but Zuras changed it to seal bond with Olympian gods. Often mistaken for [[Athena (Marvel Comics)|Athena]], [[Athens]] was built for her.  Had [[Donald & Deborah Ritter|twin children]] in affair with [[Kro (comics)|Kro]] and hid them. Became re-involved with her children, after [[Daniel Damian|Dr. Damian]] tried to kill them. Has sought sanctuary with [[Heroes For Hire]] and the [[High Evolutionary]]. She also has a fully human son named Joey Eliot.
*[[Zuras]]<ref name="The Eternals #5"/> - Can manipulate energy; Son of Kronos, brother of A'Lars, father of Thena, husband of Cybele. Was first Eternal to form [[Uni-Mind]], and was chosen to lead after Kronos' death. Was mistaken for [[Zeus (Marvel Comics)|Zeus]], so made pact with Greek gods. Killed when the Celestials defeated Uni-Mind, but continued to exist. Finally died freeing Thena from a Brain-Mine. Spirit still seems to exist, still appears occasionally in Olympia. In the 2006 series, he has been resurrected.
*[[Sprite (Eternal)|Sprite]]<ref>''The Eternals'' #9</ref> - Has the appearance of a young boy. Adept at manipulating matter. Inspiration for Puck in [[William Shakespeare|Shakespeare]]'s ''[[A Midsummer Night's Dream]]''. Sprite remained behind when Zuras called for Uni-Mind to examine the Celestials' Mothership. Is a TV star in 2006 Eternals series. 
*[[Aginar]]<ref name="The Eternals #11">''The Eternals'' #11</ref> - A Polar Eternal who disguised himself as an aide to Valkin in the guise of General Vulcanin of the Soviet Military. Doesn't care much for his Olympian Eternal "cousins".  Chosen to form the Uni-Mind which has left Earth to seek out a higher meaning for their race.
*[[Delphan Brothers]]<ref name="The Eternals #11"/> - Team of [[boxing|boxer]]s from Olympia who enjoy getting into fights. They have little love for Polar Eternals, and once started a fight with them prior to the Ritual of the Uni-Mind. When Sersi refused to answer summons to Olympia, [[Domo (comics)|Domo]] sent the Delphan Brothers to New York and force her back. She temporarily turned them into [[armadillo]]s for that.
*[[Druig]]<ref name="The Eternals #11"/> - Son of [[Valkin]], cousin of [[Ikaris]], sometimes known as "Druig of Nightmares". In modern times, Druig served as an agent of the [[K.G.B.]] in [[Russia]], enjoying the chance to inflict pain. Currently the ruler of Vorozheika, a fictional former Soviet country.
*[[Master Elo]]<ref>''Quasar'' #12</ref> - Dwells in the Himalayas. Master Elo was the Eternal who showed Makkari how to channel his powers into increased speed.
*[[Kingo Sunen]]<ref name="The Eternals #11"/> - Samurai, master swordsman, film star and producer
*[[Valkin]]<ref name="The Eternals #11"/> - Polar Eternal, father of [[Druig]], brother of [[Virako]], uncle of [[Ikaris]]. Valkin is known by some as the "All-Father", and served as the leader of the Polar Eternals. When Virako was slain, Valkin adopted Ikaris as his son. In the 20th Century, Valkin assumed the alias of "Colonel Vulcanin" in the Soviet Military. Following the death of Zuras, many Eternals turned to Valkin as their leader, even though Thena was next in line. This leadership problem was resolved when Valkin chose to lead most of Earth's Eternals into space as a Uni-Mind.
*[[Zarin]]<ref name="The Eternals #11"/> - Polar Eternal who served as an aide to Valkin in his "Colonel Vulcanin" guise. Zarin also served as Valkin's pilot. He was among the Eternals who left for space in the Uni-Mind
*[[Uni-Mind]]<ref>''The Eternals'' #12</ref> - Gestalt entity born of collective energy of participants in Ritual of the Uni-Mind. Eternals, Humans, Deviants and Brethren have been a part of Uni-Minds. Zuras was the first Eternal to form a Uni-Mind, used it to determine who should become [[Prime Eternal]]. Since then, the Prime Eternal alone has the power initiate a Uni-Mind. Following Zuras' death, a Uni-Mind left Earth containing most of the Eternals' population and apparently still exists, though Ajak, and possibly others, have left.
*The [[Forgotten One (comics)|Forgotten One]]<ref>''The Eternals'' #13</ref> - Has superhuman strength and durability greater than the norm for Eternals.  Later joined the superheroic [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]].  His true name is not known. Over the centuries he has been mistaken for [[Samson]], [[Beowulf]], [[Gilgamesh]], [[Hercules]] and [[Atlas (mythology)|Atlas]]. He also learnt to rope from [[Buffalo Bill Cody]], fought alongside [[Achilles]] in the [[Trojan War]], fought with [[King David]] in [[Kingdom of Judah|Judah]], and helped [[Aeneas]] in his travels following the Trojan War. He was also responsible for cleaning the [[Augean Stables]], rather than [[Hercules (Marvel Comics)|Hercules]]. Befriended the Interloper, but otherwise, has lived mainly in solitude from the Eternals. Was captured by a being claiming to be [[Kang the Conqueror|Kang]] and forced to train his Anachronauts, aged rapidly after he escaped.  "Kang"/[[Immortus]] then sent an Anachronaut named Neut to slay him at the Avengers' Mansion. In the 2009 series, he is living as a circus strongman, who is manipulated by Druig into attacking other Eternals.
*[[Sigmar (Marvel Comics)|Sigmar]]<ref>''The Eternals'' #17</ref> - Polar Eternal with an elderly appearance. Had at least two laboratories on Earth, one in [[Polaria (comics)|Polaria]], and one deep below [[New York City]]. Created the Molecular Reassembler, Dimension Cloud, and the Nerve Beast. Was one of the Eternals who left Earth in the form of a [[Uni-Mind]], with Phastos watching over his laboratories
*[[Khoryphos]]<ref name="Eternals vol. 2 #1 1985">''Eternals'' vol. 2 #1 (1985)</ref> - Eternal who has been mistaken for [[Orpheus]] and [[Horus]]. He is a musician, whose current composition was started during the reign of Emperor [[Tiberius]]. Met a Deviant woman, [[Yrdisis]], who he fell in love with. He is able to use his [[mandolin]] to subtely control the thoughts of other people.
*[[Cybele (comics)|Cybele]]<ref name="Eternals vol. 2 #1 1985"/> - Mother of [[Thena]] and the wife of [[Zuras]]. Cybele usually lives in a secluded forest in [[Colorado]], and does not usually take part in the affairs of her people, even though she was technically their queen while Zuras was alive. Has been mistaken for [[Gaea (comics)|Gaea]], [[Dyndymae]], [[Rhea (mythology)|Rhea]], and [[Agdistis]]. Aided the Eternals in battle against [[Ghaur]], when he gained the power of the [[Dreaming Celestial]].
*[[Phastos]]<ref name="Eternals vol. 2 #1 1985"/> - Eternal who was often mistaken for the Greek god [[Hephaestus (comics)|Hephaestus]]. Built most of the Eternals' devices, including the sword of [[Kingo Sunen]], and the flight harness used by Icarus.  Normally, he lives and works in the [[Ruhr Valley]] of [[Germany]]. Recently helped revive Virako, and serves as a member of the "New Breed" as "Ceasefire". The hammer Phastos carries has the power to manipulate machinery in ways the Eternals' own matter control powers do.
*[[Vampiro (comics)|El Vampiro]]<ref>''[[Thor (Marvel Comics)|The Mighty Thor]]'' #290</ref> - Eternal with [[Mexico|Mexican]] features who masquerades as a [[vampire]] in his role as a [[wrestler]] in [[Los Angeles]]. He has a human wife named Maria. Was prevented from answering the call to the [[Uni-Mind]] during the 4th Host when [[El Toro Rojo]] assaulted him, and injured him badly.
*[[Interloper (comics)|Interloper]]<ref>''[[Defenders (comics)|Defenders]]'' #147 (January 1986)</ref> - Polar Eternal who normally lives in seclusion in a cabin in [[Siberia]]. The only fellow Eternal that the Interloper has truly befriended is the [[Forgotten One (comics)|Forgotten One]], who has also lived a life consisting mainly of solitude. Died in battle against the [[Dragon of the Moon]] and transferred his life energies with the other Defenders, he was later resurrected by possessing the body of student Will Fanshaw, and transforming it into a replica of his own form
*[[Virako]]<ref>''[[Thor (Marvel Comics)|Thor Annual]]'' #7</ref> - Father of [[Ikaris]], brother of Valkin, husband of [[Tulayn]]. Virako is a Polar Eternal who participated in his people's missionary work to lesser cultures. He was mistaken by the Aztecs as [[Quetzalcoatl]].
*[[Arlok]]<ref>''[[What If (comics)|What If]]'' #27</ref>
*[[Astron (comics)|Astron]]<ref>''[[What If (comics)|What If]]'' #27</ref> - One of [[Uranus (mythology)|Uranus]]' followers, and leader of the Eternals of Uranus. Killed when Deathurge destroyed the dome protecting them<ref>''What If?'' vol. 1 #27</ref>
*[[Pixie (Eternal)|Pixie]]<ref>''[[Marvel: The Lost Generation]]'' #12</ref> - Member of the [[First Line (comics)|First Line]]. Can turn people to stone with "pixie dust."
*[http://www.marvunapp.com/Appendix/titaniseternal.htm Titanis]<ref name="The Eternals #1"/> - a gladiator

===Uranus===
*[[Uranos (comics)|Uranos]]<ref>''Captain Marvel'' #29 (1976)</ref> - Brutal Eternal who brought war to [[Titanos]], until he was defeated [[Oceanus (comics)|Oceanus]] and [[Kronos (comics)|Kronos]], his brother. Uranos was exiled to space with [[Uranian (comics)|24 of his followers]], who ultimately arrived upon [[Uranus]], establishing an outpost there. They next moved to [[Titan (Marvel Comics)|Titan]], after their craft was damaged by the [[Kree]]. Uranos died on Titan when his own people went to war incited by the Dragon of the Moon, but his cruelty lives through Thanos, son of his follower Sui-San
*[[Sui-San]]<ref>''[[Captain Marvel (Marvel Comics)|Captain Marvel]]'' #29 (1976)</ref> - Follower of [[Uranos]], who was the only survivor of the war on the surface of [[Titan (Marvel Comics)|Titan]]. She was found by [[A'lars]], who with her, founded the new Eternals of Titan. Sui-San's children included Eros and Thanos. She was slain by Thanos during his attack on Titan, when he dissected her with a medical kit, hoping to learn why he was different from other children.

===Titan===
*[[Kronos (comics)|Kronos]] (previously Chronos)<ref>''[[Iron Man]]'' #55</ref> - First Generation Eternal who led the rebellion against his brother Uranos. Shattered his sword to mark the new era of peace for the Eternals. With his wife Daina, had two sons, Zuras and A'Lars, but while experimenting in his lab one day, he was atomized by a blast of cosmic energy. Continues to exist as a cosmic being, one of the embodiments of time.
*Mentor ([[A'lars]])<ref> - ''Iron Man'' #55</ref> - Assumed as head of the Eternals of [[Titan (Marvel Comics)|Titan]].
*[[Thanos]]<ref name="Iron Man #55">''Iron Man'' #55</ref> - Son of [[A'lars|Mentor]] and [[Sui-San]], brother of [[Starfox (comics)|Eros]]. Is a Mutant Eternal, with purple skin and a ridged chin, plus more power than a typical Eternal of Titan, including energy blasts, psionic abilities, and teleportation. Raised the child [[Gamora]], and trained her to become the ultimate assassin.
*[[Starfox (comics)|Starfox]] ([[Eros (comics)|Eros]])<ref name="Iron Man #55"/> - Titanian Eternal, child of [[Mentor]] and [[Sui-San]] and younger brother of [[Thanos]]. Became known on Earth as a hero. Former member of the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]], under the alias "Starfox". Has the power to control the emotions of others.
*[http://www.marvunapp.com/Appendix2/demeityr.htm Demeityr]<ref>''Solo Avengers'' #18 (May, 1989)</ref>
