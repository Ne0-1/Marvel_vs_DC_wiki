'''Gloria Grant''', more commonly known as '''Glory Grant''', is a [[Marvel Comics]] supporting character of [[Spider-Man]]. She is introduced in ''[[The Amazing Spider-Man]]'' #140 (January 1975) as a neighbor of Peter Parker. Peter then helps her secure a position as [[J. Jonah Jameson]]'s secretary at the ''[[Daily Bugle]]'', replacing [[Betty Brant]].

==Fictional character biography==
When first introduced, Glory Grant is pursuing a modeling career and meets ''Daily Bugle'' photographer [[Spider-Man|Peter Parker]] who has just moved in to her Lower West Side apartment building.{{issue|date=August 2013}} They then become fast friends.<ref>''Amazing Spider-Man'' #163</ref> When Glory is looking for work, Peter brought her to the [[Daily Bugle]] where the publisher [[J. Jonah Jameson]] has been going through secretaries since the departure of his long-time secretary [[Betty Brant]] . She works for Jameson and later [[Robbie Robertson (comics)|Robbie Robertson]] who becomes editor-in-chief.<ref>''Spectacular Spider-Man'' #2</ref>

Grant falls in love with a gang boss named Eduardo Lobo. He and his brother Carlos wage a gang war against the [[Kingpin (comics)|Kingpin]], and he uses Glory to access the Daily Bugle's research files on the Kingpin. However, he falls in love with her, and Glory is conflicted over the romance. Peter suggests she follow her heart. When Eduardo ends up battling Spider -Man, Glory shoots and kills Eduardo. Spider-Man thanks her but she reveals that she was aiming at Spider-Man, having followed her heart as Peter suggested.<ref>''Spectacular Spider-Man'' #146 & 149-154/''Web of Spider-Man'' #47-48, 51 & 55, 1989</ref>

Despite Glory harboring a deep resentment for Spider-Man he helps her and government agent [[Shotgun (comics)|Shotgun]] against the late [[Haitian Vodou|voodoo]] witch [[Calypso (comics)|Calypso]], who spiritually possesses Grant long enough to engineer a scheme that brings Calypso fully back from the dead.<ref>''Spider-Man Annual '97'', 1997</ref> 

When Jameson becomes Mayor, Grant becomes one of his aides, appearing with him at the [[The Raft (comics)|Raft]] on the day of [[Alistair Smythe]]'s execution.<ref>''The Superior Spider-Man'' #11</ref> However, she later quit his administration when she saw that Jameson would never end his personal vendetta against Spider-Man.<ref>''The Superior Spider-Man'' #29</ref>

==Other versions==
* In the [[Marvel Noir]] Universe, Glory appears in Spider-Man: Eyes without a Face. She appears in a relatively minor role, being the girlfriend of [[Robbie Robertson (comics)|Robbie Robertson]], and accompanying Joseph's father to the Parkers when he goes missing.  She is again seen at the end devastated at the fact that Robertson has been lobotomised.<ref>''Spider-Man Noir: Eyes Without a Mask Issues #2, #4''</ref>

* In the universe of ''[[Spider-Gwen]]'', a teenage version of Glory is seen as a friend and band mate of [[Gwen Stacy]].<ref>''Spider-Gwen'' #2</ref>

==In other media==
===Television===
* On the CBS live-action [[Amazing Spider-Man (TV series)|''Amazing Spider-Man'']] TV series of the 1970s, a woman resembled her named "Rita Conway" was played by [[Chip Fields]] (mother of [[Kim Fields]]).

* Glory Grant first appears in the 1994's ''[[Spider-Man (1994 TV series)|Spider-Man]]'' episode "[[Day of the Chameleon]]" voiced by [[Nell Carter]]. In this show, she is the secretary of the [[Daily Bugle]].

* Glory Grant appears in ''[[The Spectacular Spider-Man (TV series)|The Spectacular Spider-Man]]'' TV series voiced by [[Cree Summer]]. She first appeared in the episode "[[Competition (The Spectacular Spider-Man)|Competition]]". She attends Midtown High with Peter Parker and is [[Kenny McFarlane|Kenny Kong]]'s girlfriend. She breaks up with him for being too childish and then shows interest towards [[Harry Osborn]], even going as far as going to the fall formal with him. Kenny and Glory got back together at the fall formal, humiliating Harry. Though she becomes worried about him when he disappeared from the formal (Harry was dousing the "[[Green Goblin|Globulin Green]]"). During Season Two, Glory appears to be getting annoyed by Kenny again.
