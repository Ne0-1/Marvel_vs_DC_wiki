'''Al MacKenzie''' is a [[fictional character]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].

==Publication history==
Al MacKenzie first appeared in ''Nick Fury vs. S.H.I.E.L.D.'' #3 (August 1988), and was created by [[Bob Harras]] and [[Paul Neary]].

The character subsequently appears in ''Nick Fury, Agent of S.H.I.E.L.D.'' #1-7 (September 1989-January 1990).

Al MacKenzie received an entry in the ''[[Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe|Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe Update '89]]'' #4.

==Fictional character biography==
Al MacKenzie was born in [[Austin, Texas]]. He was once the [[Central Intelligence Agency|CIA]] liaison to [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]]{{issue|date=December 2011}}  He became romantically involved with Contessa [[Valentina Allegra di Fontaine]] after a while, which led to an estrangement between him and [[Nick Fury]].{{issue|date=December 2011}}  Because of this, he returned to the CIA with the Contessa.{{issue|date=December 2011}}

MacKenzie later joined the S.H.I.E.L.D. organization full-time, and spent considerable time as the senior liaison officer to the C.I.A.{{issue|date=December 2011}} Subsequently, under unknown circumstances, MacKenzie resigned from S.H.I.E.L.D., and wrote a "tell-all" book entitled ''UnSHIELDed: an Unauthorized Insider's Look Behind the World's Most Powerful Global Spy Network'', which purportedly explained some of the history behind the organization from his point of view.<ref>''[[Amazing Fantasy]]'' Vol. 2, # 10</ref> Since then, he also acted as an unofficial source to [[Ben Urich]], prodding Urich and [[Jessica Jones]] to attempt to expose Fury's unauthorized mission to [[Latveria]].<ref>''[[The Pulse (comics)|The Pulse]]'' # 9</ref>

==In other media==

===Television===
* Alphonso "Mack" Mackenzie appears in ''[[Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.]]'', portrayed by [[Henry Simmons]].{{cn|date=October 2015}} He is depicted as an African-American [[mechanic]] and [[engineer]] who is recruited into the newly rebuilt S.H.I.E.L.D. after the events of ''[[Captain America: The Winter Soldier]]''. He is also old friends with [[Mockingbird (Marvel Comics)|Barbara "Bobbi" Morse]]. In the episode "One of Us," it is revealed that he and Bobbi are secretly associated with "the real S.H.I.E.L.D." In the episode "One Door Closes," it was revealed that Mack was on the S.H.I.E.L.D. aircraft carrier ''Iliad'' at the time when the Hydra infiltrators made their move. He and Robert Gonzales were saved by Bobbi Morse and Isabelle Hartley. Mackenzie, though close friends with the [[Inhuman]] S.H.I.E.L.D. agent [[Daisy Johnson]], is quite distrustful of Inhumans. In the episode "Closure", S.H.I.E.L.D. director [[Phil Coulson]] appoints Mack as interim director.
