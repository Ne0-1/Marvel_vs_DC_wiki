'''Flying Tiger''' is a [[fictional]] [[Marvel Comics]] supervillain.

==Publication history==
Flying Tiger first appeared in ''[[Spider-Woman (Jessica Drew)|Spider-Woman]] #40'' (October 1981), and was created by [[Chris Claremont]] and [[Steve Leialoha]].

The character subsequently appears in ''Spider-Woman'' #50 (June 1983), ''Iron Man'' #177 (December 1983), ''Fantastic Four'' #335 (December 1989), ''Avengers Spotlight'' #29 (February 1990), ''Captain America'' #411 (January 1993), #413 (March 1993), ''Thunderbolts'' #3 (June 1997), #18-20 (September–November 1998), #22 (January 1999), #24-25 (March–April 1999), and ''Avengers: The Initiative Annual'' #1 (January 2008).

Flying Tiger received an entry in the ''All-New Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe A to Z: Update'' #2 (2007).

==Fictional character biography==
Flying Tiger's true identity is unknown. A professional football player whose career was cut short, he developed a costume that enabled him to fly.

He started his criminal career as a mercenary assassin when he was hired to kill the original [[Spider-Woman (Jessica Drew)|Spider-Woman]].<ref>''Spider-Woman'' #40</ref> He was later hired to kill businessmen Regis Fussky but was stopped from doing so by [[Iron Man]]/[[War Machine|Jim Rhodes]].<ref>''Iron Man'' #177</ref>

During the ''[[Acts of Vengeance]]'', [[Doctor Doom]] used an Aggression Enhancer on Flying Tiger and other villains to attack the [[Fantastic Four]] when they stood before congress. They were defeated by the Fantastic Four.<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #335</ref> Flying Tiger was next seen being admitted into the [[Vault (comics)|Vault]] following a massive prison escape attempt.<ref>''Avengers Spotlight'' #29</ref> Later, Flying Tiger joined the [[Crimson Cowl (Justine Hammer)|Crimson Cowl's]] [[Masters of Evil]]. The group's plot to blackmail the governments of the world using a weather-controlling machine was stopped by the [[Thunderbolts (comics)|Thunderbolts]].<ref>''Thunderbolts'' #18-25</ref>

Later in San Francisco, Flying Tiger was defeated by [[Armory (comics)|Armory]].<ref>''Avengers: The Initiative Annual'' #1/2</ref>

During the ''[[Fear Itself (comics)|Fear Itself]]'' storyline, Flying Tiger was seen trying to escape from the demolished Raft until he ran into the [[Avengers Academy]] staff.<ref>''Avengers Academy'' #15</ref>

==Equipment==
Flying Tiger wears body armor under a tiger costume, and is capable of powered flight. He has enhanced strength, durability, endurance and a set of claws.
