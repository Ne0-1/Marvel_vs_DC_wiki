The '''Wrecker''' ('''Dirk Garthwaite''') is a [[fictional character]] that appears in [[comic books]] published by [[Marvel Comics]]. The Wrecker was created by [[Stan Lee]] and [[Jack Kirby]] and first appears in ''[[Thor (Marvel Comics)|The Mighty Thor]]'' #148 (Jan. 1968).

==Publication history==
Dirk Garthwaite first appears in the title ''Thor'', and is depicted as a former [[manual laborer]] in a [[demolition]]s crew who is fired for his violent and anti-social tendencies. Garthwaite creates a [[costume]] for himself and adopting the alias the ''Wrecker'', commits a series of [[robberies]], demolishing looted locations and leaving a [[crowbar]] at the scene of the crime. The Wrecker enters a [[hotel]] room that, unknown to him, is occupied by [[Asgardian (comics)|Asgardian]] god [[Loki (comics)|Loki]], the [[adopted]] brother and arch-foe of the Thunder God [[Thor (Marvel Comics)|Thor]]. The Wrecker accidentally receives an [[enchantment]] - meant for Loki - from Loki's ally, [[Norn]] Queen [[Karnilla]], and courtesy of his now [[indestructible]] crowbar is granted superhuman strength and stamina. 

Reveling in his new-found power, the Wrecker embarks on a crime spree and also defeats Thor, who at the time has had his power severely reduced by father [[Odin (Marvel Comics)|Odin]] (ruler of the [[Norse mythology|Norse]] gods) as punishment for choosing to remain on Earth. The Wrecker is defeated by the female Asgardian warrior [[Sif (Marvel Comics)|Sif]], who animates the [[armor]] of the Asgardian [[Destroyer (Thor)|Destroyer]] to save Thor.<ref>''Thor'' #148 - 150 (Jan. - Mar. 1968)</ref> 

The character returns to the title ''Thor'' and battles the Thunder God once again, although a fully restored Thor easily defeats the villain.<ref>''Thor'' #171 (Dec. 1969)</ref> 

The Wrecker returns in the title ''Defenders'', and on this occasion appears with three superpowered partners in crime: [[Thunderball (comics)|Thunderball]]; [[Bulldozer (comics)|Bulldozer]] and [[Piledriver (comics)|Piledriver]]. Empowered when holding the crowbar and it is struck by [[lightning]], the villains join the Wrecker to become the [[Wrecking Crew (comics)|Wrecking Crew]]. While searching for a [[gamma bomb]] built by Thunderball - with which they hope to [[extort]] [[New York City]] - the Wrecking Crew encounter and battle several members of the Defenders: [[Doctor Strange]]; [[Nighthawk (Marvel Comics)|Nighthawk]] and the [[Hulk (comics)|Hulk]]. Aided by [[Luke Cage|Power Man]], the heroes defeat the villains.<ref>''Defenders'' #17 - 19 (Nov. 1974 - Jan. 1975)</ref>

After an appearance in title ''[[Fantastic Four]]'' as the pawn of villain the [[Puppet Master (comics)|Puppet Master]],<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #168 (Mar. 1976)</ref> the Wrecker and the Crew feature in the title ''[[Iron Fist (comics)|Iron Fist]]'' in a battle against heroes Iron Fist and [[Captain America]].<ref>''Iron Fist'' #11 - 12 (Feb. & Apr. 1977)</ref> The Wrecking Crew reappear in ''Thor'' against the Thunder God, but are quickly beaten.<ref>''Thor'' #304 (Feb. 1981)</ref>

The Wrecker appears with the Wrecking Crew and other villains in the [[limited series]] ''[[Secret Wars]]'';<ref>''Secret Wars'' #1 - 12 (May 1984 - Apr. 1985)</ref> battles ''[[Spider-Man]]'' and team mate Thunderball over control of the Norn power in the title ''[[Spectacular Spider-Man]]'';<ref>''Spectacular Spider-Man'' #125 - 126 (Apr. - May 1987)</ref> and joins the fourth incarnation of supervillain team the [[Masters of Evil]] in a raid on [[Avengers Mansion]]. With the Wrecking Crew and other villains, the Wrecker savagely beats Avenger [[Hercules (Marvel Comics)|Hercules]]<ref>''Avengers'' #274 (Dec. 1986)</ref> before being captured the remaining Avengers.<ref>''Avengers'' #270 - 277 (Aug. 1986 - Mar. 1987)</ref> The character features in the title ''[[Iron Man]]'' during the [[Acts of Vengeance]] storyline;<ref>''Iron Man'' #251 (Dec. 1989)</ref> and in ''Thor'' has several battles with the Wrecking Crew against Thor and allies Hercules<ref name="Thor #418 June 1990">''Thor'' #418 (June 1990)</ref> superhero team [[Excalibur (comics)|Excalibur]]<ref>''Thor'' #428 (Jan. 1991)</ref> and [[Ghost Rider (Daniel Ketch)|Ghost Rider]]<ref>''Thor'' #430 (Mar. 1991)</ref> before being defeated.<ref>''Thor'' #431 (Apr. 1991)</ref> The character also receives instruction from another opponent of Thor, the Asgardian [[troll]] [[Ulik]], as to how to utilize the full power of the enchanted crowbar.<ref name="Thor #418 June 1990" />

After an appearance with other villains in the title ''Captain America'',<ref>''Captain America'' #412 - 413 (Feb. - Mar. 1993)</ref> and with the Wrecking Crew in ''[[Alpha Flight]]'',<ref>''Alpha Flight'' #118 - 119 (Mar. - Apr. 1993)</ref> the Wrecker features in ''[[Thunderstrike]]''<ref>''Thunderstrike'' #13 - 14 (Oct. - Nov. 1994)</ref> and reunites with the Wrecking Crew in ''[[Journey Into Mystery]]''.<ref>''Journey Into Mystery'' #505 (Jan. 1997)</ref> After an appearance in [[flashback]] in the first issue of ''[[Thunderbolts]]'',<ref>''Thunderbolts'' #1 (Apr. 1997)</ref> the character appears in the second volume of ''[[Marvel Team-Up]]'' against Spider-Man, [[Namor the Sub-Mariner]]; [[Doctor Strange]] and Iron Man,<ref>''Marvel Team-Up'' vol. 2, #6 (Feb. 1998); #8 (Apr. 1998) & #11 (July 1998)</ref> makes an appearance in ''Thunderbolts''<ref>''Thunderbolts'' #22 (Jan. 1999)</ref> and with the Wrecking Crew battles the Avengers.<ref>''Avengers'' #16 - 18 (May - July 1999)</ref>

With the Wrecking Crew the character features in ''Thor'' and battles the [[Warriors Three]] the Thunder God;<ref>''Thor'' #28 (Oct. 2000)</ref> has a brief appearance in the title ''[[Wolverine (comics)|Wolverine]]''<ref>''Wolverine'' #164 (July 2001)</ref> and then ''Avengers'', which features a grueling battle that leaves a [[civilian]] dead.<ref>''Avengers'' #77 - 80 (Mar. - May 2004)</ref> The Wrecker appears in the title ''[[She-Hulk]]'';<ref>''She-Hulk'' #5 - 6 (Sep. - Oct. 2004) & #10 (Feb. 2005)</ref> in flashback in ''Avengers Finale''<ref>''Avengers Finale'' #1 (Jan. 2005))</ref> and the ''[[New Thunderbolts]]''.<ref>''New Thunderbolts'' #2 (Jan. 2005)</ref> The character becomes a perennial foe for the Avengers and features in the title ''[[New Avengers]]'';<ref>''New Avengers'' #7 - 8 (July - Aug. 2005)</ref> ''New Avengers: Most Wanted Files'';<ref>''New Avengers: Most Wanted Files'' #1 (Dec. 2005)</ref> briefly in ''[[New Excalibur]]''<ref>''New Excalbur'' #13 (Jan. 2007)</ref> and with the Wrecking Crew battles [[Canadian]] superhero team ''[[Omega Flight]]'' in the title of the same name.<ref>''Omega Flight'' #1 - 5 (June - Oct 2007)</ref> 

After an appearance in the second volume of ''She-Hulk'',<ref>''She-Hulk'' vol. 2, #21 (Oct. 2007)</ref> the title ''New Avengers'' reveals that villain the [[Hood (comics)|Hood]] has hired a small army of criminals, which includes the Wrecker (taking advantage of the division in the superhero community caused by the [[Registration acts (comics)#2006 Superhuman Registration Act|Superhuman Registration Act]]<ref>''New Avengers'' #35 (Oct. 2007)</ref> In the title ''[[Daredevil (Marvel Comics)|Daredevil]]'' and at the Hood's direction, the Wrecker battles fellow criminals the [[Enforcers (comics)|Enforcers]]<ref>''Daredevil'' vol. 2, #102 (Jan. 2008)</ref> and the New Avengers.<ref>''New Avengers'' #37 & ''New Avengers'' Annual #2 (Feb. 2008)</ref> The character and the Wrecking Crew feature in a short story in the second volume of ''Marvel Comics Presents'';<ref>''Marvel Comics Presents'' vol. 2, #5 (Mar. 2008)</ref> then in ''[[Punisher War Journal]]'';<ref>''Punisher War Journal'' #22 - 23 (Oct. - Nov. 2008)</ref> the limited series ''[[1985]]''<ref>''1985'' #6 (Nov. 2008):''1985'' #1 - 6 (July - Nov. 2008)</ref> and with the Hood's army appear in the [[Secret Invasion]] storyline and battle the [[Extraterrestrial life|alien]] [[Skrull]] force invading [[New York City]].<ref>''Secret Invasion'' #1 - 8 (June 2008 - Jan. 2009)</ref> 

The character continues to make appearances across several titles as an employee of the Hood, including ''New Avengers'';<ref>''New Avengers'' #46 (Dec. 2008)</ref> ''Captain America'';<ref>''Captain America'' vol. 5, #43 (Dec. 2008)</ref> limited series ''[[Marvel Apes]]''<ref>''Marvel Apes'' #4 (Dec. 2008): ''Marvel Apes'' #0 (Jan. 2008); #1 - 2 (Nov. 2008); #3 - 4 (Dec. 2008)</ref> and a promotional comic titled ''Thor'', produced by [[fast food]] chain [[Taco Bell]].<ref>''Taco Bell Exclusive Collector Edition: Thor'' #1 (Jan. 2009)</ref> Together with the Wrecking Crew the character features in the [[Parallel universe (fiction)|alternate universe]] title ''[[What If (comics)|What If?]]'';<ref>''What If?: House of M'' & ''What If?: Secret Wars'' #1 (Feb. 2009)</ref> ''Dark Reign Files'';<ref>''Dark Reign Files'' #1 (Apr. 2009)</ref> in flashback in ''[[Incredible Hercules]]''<ref>''Incredible Hercules''  #126 (Apr. 2009)</ref> and stories that form part of the [[Dark Reign (comics)|Dark Reign]] storyline.<ref>''New Avengers'' #50 (Apr. 2009); ''Dark Reign: The Cabal'' #1 (June 2009); ''Dark Reign: The Hood'' #1 - 2 (July - Aug. 2009) & ''New Avengers'' #55 (Sep. 2009)</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Courtesy of an Asgardian enchantment on a crowbar, Dirk Garthwaite possesses superhuman strength, stamina and durability ([[bulletproof]]). When he initially shares the Norn power with the Wrecking Crew, the Wrecker's abilities are reduced. After training from Ulik the troll, the Wrecker no longer suffers this handicap, and is now capable of using the crowbar in the same fashion that Thor can utilize the mystic hammer [[Mjolnir (comics)|Mjolnir]]. The crowbar's abilities include  absorbing and projecting energy; creating [[illusion]]s; generating a [[force field]]; creating minor [[earthquake]]s; [[teleportation]] and returning to the Wrecker when thrown. The Wrecker also shares a mental link with the crowbar, and can mentally control any weak-minded individual touching the object.
