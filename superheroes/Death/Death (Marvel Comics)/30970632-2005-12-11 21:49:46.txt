'''Death''' is a [[fictional character]], a [[death (personification)|personification of death]] in the [[Marvel Comics]] [[Marvel Universe|universe]].  This version of Death is usually portrayed as a fair-skinned [[woman]] or a [[human skeleton|skeleton]], wearing a diaphanous [[purple]] [[robe]].  [[Thanos]], the mad Titan, is obsessed with her, and has embarked on any number of mad schemes to win her love.  During the ''[[Contest of Champions]]'', she engaged in a contest by proxy with the [[Grandmaster (Marvel Comics)|Grandmaster]], and lost; angered, she refused his fellow [[Elders of the Universe]] from ever knowing the peace of death.
[[Image:Deathmarvelcomics.jpg|150px|Death with Thanos and Captain Marvel in a scene from The Death of Captain Marvel. Art by Jim Starlin.|left|thumb]]
Death's siblings are the cosmic entities [[Eternity (comics)|Eternity]], [[Infinity (Marvel Comics)|Infinity]] and Oblivion. Death has one child with Thanos, the Rot. It's said that Death, Eternity and [[Galactus|Galactus]] are the three fundamental entities in the Marvel Comics Universe.

Death has a romantic attachment to the mercenary [[Deadpool]], much to the chagrin of Thanos.

Contrast with [[Death (Sandman)|Death]], of [[DC Comics]]' ''[[The Sandman (DC Comics Modern Age)|Sandman]]''.
