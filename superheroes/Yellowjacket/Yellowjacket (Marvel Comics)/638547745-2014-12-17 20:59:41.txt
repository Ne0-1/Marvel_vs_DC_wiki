'''Yellowjacket''' is the name of several [[fictional]] [[superheroes]] and [[super heroines]] appearing in [[comic books]] published by [[Marvel Comics]]. Most of these versions exist in Marvel's main [[shared universe]], known as the [[Marvel Universe]]. [[Corey Stoll]] will portray an adaptation of Yellowjacket in the 2015 film ''[[Ant-Man (film)|Ant-Man]]''.

==Hank Pym==
{{main|Hank Pym}}

'''Hank Pym''' is the first character to take on the Yellowjacket code name in the modern mainstream Marvel Comics.  Pym was also known as [[Ant-Man]], Giant-Man, [[Goliath (comics)|Goliath]] and later as the [[Wasp (comics)|Wasp]].  The character has been associated with several superhero teams in the Marvel Universe, including [[Avengers (comics)|the Avengers]] and [[Defenders (comics)|the Defenders]].

==Rita DeMara==
{{main|Yellowjacket (Rita DeMara)}}

'''Rita DeMara''' is the second Yellowjacket in the modern mainstream comics who debuted as a [[supervillainess]].  She joined the supervillain groups known as the [[Masters of Evil]] and the [[Femizons]]. DeMara later became a [[superheroine]] and joined the ranks of the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]] and the [[Guardians of the Galaxy (1969 team)|Guardians of the Galaxy]].  She was killed by [[Iron Man]] who was under the control of the super villain [[Immortus]].<ref>''Avengers: The Crossing'' #1</ref>  She was later resurrected in the ''[[Chaos War (comics)|Chaos War]]'' storyline.<ref>''Chaos War'' #2</ref>

==Other versions==

===Earth-1610===
The [[Ultimate Marvel]] version of ''Yellowjacket'' is [[Ultron]]. Henry Pym creates android duplicates of the Ultimates.  Ultron, who has fallen in love with the [[Scarlet Witch]] but is rebuffed by her, takes control of the Yellowjacket android to murder her.<ref>''Ultimates 3'' #1 - 4 (Feb. - May 2008); #5 (Nov. 2008)</ref>
