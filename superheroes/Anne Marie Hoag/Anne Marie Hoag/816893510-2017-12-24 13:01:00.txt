'''Anne Marie Hoag''' is a fictional character appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].

==Publication history==
{{expand-section|date=July 2017}}
The character, created by [[Dwayne McDuffie]], first appeared in ''[[Marvel Comics Presents]]'' #19 (May 1989).

==Fictional character biography==
Anne Marie Hoag is the enigmatic owner of [[Damage Control (comics)|Damage Control]]. How she achieved this position as well as her history remain a mystery. As stated in ''Damage Control'' Vol. 2 #2, "[She] does not believe that one's history should be available for public consumption." Ms. Hoag started Damage Control with funding from [[Iron Man|Tony Stark]] and [[Kingpin (comics)|Wilson Fisk]], with the former feeling uneasy at this alliance.<ref>''Damage Control'' Vol. 2 #1</ref> She has a tough and intimidating personality and despite her mean exterior, deeply cared for her employees. At one point, Ms. Hoag accepted a job from the government and nominated [[Robin Chapel (comics)|Robin Chapel]] as her replacement. The company nearly fell apart and Hoag convinced [[Nick Fury]] and [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]] to invest in the company.<ref>''Damage Control'' Vol. 2 #4</ref> She nearly lost her job to the unscrupulous Walter Declun, but with [[Wolverine (character)|Wolverine]]'s help she gained control of Damage Control.<ref>''Wolverine'' Vol. 3 #46-47</ref>
