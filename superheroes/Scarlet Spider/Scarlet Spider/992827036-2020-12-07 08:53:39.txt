The '''Scarlet Spider''' is an alias used by several [[Character (arts)|fictional characters]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].

==Fictional character biography==
===Ben Reilly===
{{Main|Ben Reilly}}
Benjamin "Ben" Reilly, the first major version of the Scarlet Spider, is a clone of [[Spider-Man]] created by the [[Jackal (Marvel Comics)|Jackal]].<ref>''The Amazing Spider-Man'' #149-151</ref> He first appeared as the Scarlet Spider in ''[[Web of Spider-Man]]'' #118 (Nov 1994).<ref>{{cite book |last1=Brevoort |first1=Tom |last2=DeFalco |first2=Tom |last3=Manning |first3=Matthew K. |last4=Sanderson |first4=Peter |last5=Wiacek |first5=Win |title=Marvel Year By Year: A Visual History |date=2017 |publisher=DK Publishing |isbn=978-1465455505 |page=271}}</ref>

===Peter Parker===
{{main|Spider-Man}}
Peter Parker was forced to use the Scarlet Spider identity due to all of his Spider-Man costumes being ruined to continue his superheroic activities, while Ben Reilly pretended to be the former in prison.{{Issue|date=August 2018}}

===Joe Wade===
'''Joe Wade''' is the only character to operate as a villain under the Scarlet Spider alias. An undercover FBI agent assigned to investigate the second [[Lady Octopus|Doctor Octopus]],<ref>''The Spectacular Scarlet Spider'' #1</ref> However, the villain discovers Joe and traps his body in a virtual reality chamber before using his thoughts to power a hard-light holographic duplicate of Ben Reilly to tarnish his name.<ref>''Scarlet Spider'' #2</ref> Despite this, Joe is unable to stop himself from committing acts of violence. When the real Ben Reilly attacks Doctor Octopus' lair, he damages the machine while Joe is still inside, <ref>''The Spectacular Scarlet Spider'' #2</ref> turning Joe into a mechanized version of the Scarlet Spider with superhuman strength and speed, claws on his fingertips, the ability to fire webbing from his wrists, crawl up walls, and fire laser "stingers" from his eyes. After he goes on a rampage, Ben Reilly joins forces with the [[New Warriors]] to stop the cybernetic Scarlet Spider before the FBI put him in custody so Joe can undergo medical treatment to remove the technology.<ref>''Web of Scarlet Spider'' #3</ref><ref>''New Warriors'' #67</ref><ref>''Web of Scarlet Spider'' #4</ref>

===Scarlet Spiders (Red Team)===
{{Main|Michael Van Patrick}}
The Scarlet Spiders, secretly all clones of Michael Van Patrick, work with the [[Avengers: The Initiative|Initiative]] and wear advanced versions of the [[Iron Spider]] armor.

===Kaine Parker===
{{Main|Kaine Parker}}
Kaine Parker, a clone of Spider-Man also created by the Jackal, is the fifth major version of the Scarlet Spider.

==Other versions==
===MC2===
[[Image:FelicityScarlet.jpg|right|180px|thumb|Felicity Hardy makes her debut as Scarlet Spider. Art by [[Pat Olliffe]].]]
In the alternate future [[Marvel Comics 2|MC2]], '''Felicity Hardy''', daughter of [[Black Cat (Marvel Comics)|Felicia Hardy]] and [[Flash Thompson]], adopts the Scarlet Spider identity to irritate her mother. She attempts to convince [[Spider-Girl (Mayday Parker)|Spider-Girl]] to take her on as a sidekick, but the latter refuses.<ref>''Spider-Girl'' #45-46 (June 2002)</ref> Undeterred, she continues to fight crime until several near-death experiences cause her to give up the identity.  Although she has no actual powers, she is skilled in martial arts and gymnastics and utilizes an array of spider-themed weaponry.

===''Spider-Gwen''===
The ''[[Spider-Gwen]]'' universe's [[Mary Jane Watson#Spider-Gwen|Mary Jane Watson]] dresses as the Scarlet Spider for Halloween.<ref>''Spider-Gwen'' (vol. 2) #13</ref>

==In other media==

===Television===
* A shadowed version of the Scarlet Spider cameos in the 1990s ''[[Fantastic Four (1994 TV series)|Fantastic Four]]'' animated series.<ref>[https://www.cbr.com/scarlet-spiders-debut-wasnt-in-spider-man/ "The Scarlet Spider's '90s Animated Debut Wasn't in an Episode of Spider-Man" - CBR.com]</ref>
* The Ben Reilly incarnation of the Scarlet Spider appears in the 1990s ''[[Spider-Man (1994 TV series)|Spider-Man]]'' animated series, voiced by [[Christopher Daniel Barnes]].
* Two versions of the Scarlet Spider appear in the ''[[Ultimate Spider-Man (TV series)|Ultimate Spider-Man]]'' animated series. The first version is [[Flash Thompson]] using a wannabe superhero identity in ''Ultimate Spider-Man: Web Warriors''. The second version is a recurring character in the fourth season, ''Ultimate Spider-Man vs. the Sinister Six'', and is a hybridization of Ben Reilly's loyalty and dedication, and Kaine Parker's original comics suit and personality.<ref>{{cite episode|title=Hydra Attacks Pt. 2|series=Ultimate Spider-Man|network=Disney XD|season=4|number=2|airdate=February 21, 2016}}</ref>

===Video games===
* The Ben Reilly incarnation of the Scarlet Spider appears as an alternate costume for Spider-Man (Peter Parker) in the 2000 ''[[Spider-Man (2000 video game)|Spider-Man]]'' video game, ''[[Spider-Man 2: Enter Electro]]'', ''[[Marvel: Ultimate Alliance]]'', ''[[Spider-Man: Shattered Dimensions]]'', ''[[Ultimate Marvel vs. Capcom 3]]'', and ''[[Spider-Man: Edge of Time]]''.
* The Ben Reilly incarnation of the Scarlet Spider appears as a playable character in ''[[Marvel Super Hero Squad Online]]'', voiced by [[Chris Cox (voice actor)|Chris Cox]]. 
* The Kaine Parker incarnation of the Scarlet Spider appears as alternate costume for Spider-Man (Peter Parker) in ''[[The Amazing Spider-Man (2012 video game)|The Amazing Spider-Man]]''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.justpushstart.com/2012/06/scarlet-spider-costume-announced-for-the-amazing-spider-man/|title=Scarlet Spider Costume Announced For The Amazing Spider-Man|publisher=Just Push Start|date=23 June 2012}}</ref> and ''[[The Amazing Spider-Man 2 (2014 video game)|The Amazing Spider-Man 2]]'' video games.
* The Ben Reilly, Kaine Parker, Jon Wade, and Felicity Hardy incarnations of the Scarlet Spider appear as playable characters in ''[[Spider-Man Unlimited (video game)|Spider-Man Unlimited]]''.
* The Ben Reilly incarnation of the Scarlet Spider appears as a playable character in ''[[Lego Marvel Super Heroes 2]]''.
* The Ben Reilly and Kaine Parker incarnations of the Scarlet Spider appear as alternate costumes for Spider-Man (Peter Parker) in the 2018 ''[[Spider-Man (2018 video game)|Spider-Man]]'' video game.
* The Ben Reilly incarnation of the Scarlet Spider appears as a playable character in ''[[Marvel Future Fight]]''.
