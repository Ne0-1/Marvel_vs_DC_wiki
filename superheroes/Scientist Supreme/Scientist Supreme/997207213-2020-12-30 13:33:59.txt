'''Scientist Supreme''' is a name used by different fictional characters appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].

==Description==
The Scientist Supreme is often described to be the top leader of [[Advanced Idea Mechanics|A.I.M.]], and can also be described to be the scientific counterpart to Earth's Sorcerer Supreme.

==Known versions==
===Yandroth===
{{main|Yandroth}}
Yandroth was the humanoid Scientist Supreme of the otherdimensional planet Yann, and a counterpart to [[Doctor Strange]]'s title of Sorcerer Supreme.<ref>''Strange Tales'' Vol. 1 #164 (January 1968). Marvel Comics.</ref>

===Hank Pym===
{{main|Hank Pym}}

'''Andrew Forson''' is the sixth character within A.I.M. to use the title of Scientist Supreme. The character first appeared in ''[[Fantastic Four (comic book)|Fantastic Four]]'' #610 and was created by [[Jonathan Hickman]] and [[Ryan Stegman]].

Forson was the Scientist Supreme of A.I.M. after the organization became public and established their base in the now-sovereign island nation of [[Barbuda]] and overthrew the [[Wizard (Marvel Comics)|Wizard]] with consent of the rest of A.I.M.. Barbuda was then renamed A.I.M. Island.<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #610 (November 2012). Marvel Comics.</ref>

Forson appears as the Supreme Leader of the new High Council of A.I.M., consisting of [[Graviton (comics)|Graviton]] (as the Minister of Science), Jude the Entropic Man (as the [[Health minister|Minister of Health]]), [[Mentallo]] (as the Minister of Home Affairs), [[Superia]] (as the [[Education minister|Minister of Education]] in Bagalia), the female [[Black Widow (Yelena Belova)|Super-Adaptoid]] (as the [[Minister of State]] in Bagalia), and the undercover [[Taskmaster (comics)|Taskmaster]] (as the [[Defence minister|Minister of Defense]]).<ref>''Secret Avengers'' vol. 2 #2 (March 2013). Marvel Comics.</ref> Forson then leads A.I.M. into attending a weapons expo, which led to A.I.M. fighting against the [[Secret Avengers]]. During the battle, Forson takes the opportunity to steal the [[Iron Patriot]] armor.<ref>''Secret Avengers'' vol. 2 #3 (April 2013). Marvel Comics.</ref> [[Daisy Johnson]] launched an unsanctioned operation to send the Secret Avengers to A.I.M. Island to assassinate Forson, and the group seemingly killed him. Despite Johnson ending up suspended for breaking protocol and [[Maria Hill]] being put in charge of S.H.I.E.L.D. again, Forson was revealed to be alive all along, and the news of A.I.M. being a new permanent member of the Security Council is known.<ref>''Secret Avengers'' vol. 2 #5 (June 2013). Marvel Comics.</ref>

Forson finds Superia badly injured after an incident on A.I.M. Island regarding an escaped creature.<ref>''Avengers'' vol. 5 #15. Marvel Comics.</ref> An A.I.M. Agent then approaches Forson and Superia, showing the two a 2-D hologram of the organism beating the Avengers. Taking pride in this, Forson orders their translocator activated and to retrieve their "lost child".<ref>''Avengers'' vol. 5 #16 (July 2013). Marvel Comics.</ref> When Taskmaster is shot and seemingly killed by Mockingbird now under Forson's control,<ref>''Secret Avengers'' vol. 2 #13 (February 2014). Marvel Comics.</ref> Forson revealed that Mockingbird was secretly working for Forson years ago, and that he used Mockingbird to "get [[Victorius (comics)|Victorious]] out of the way" so that he could take over the Cult of Entropy.<ref>''Secret Avengers'' vol. 2 #14 (March 2014). Marvel Comics.</ref> Forson also uses [[Super-Adaptoid|Adaptoids]] from an alternate reality to combat the Avengers.<ref>''Avengers'' vol. 5 #25-28 (January–April 2014). Marvel Comics.</ref> Using an unidentified device, Forson and A.I.M. accelerate the flow of time in the limits of A.I.M. Island, creating in a matter of hours for the real world year of progress and transforming A.I.M. into a technologically advanced empire.<ref>''Avengers World'' #1. Marvel Comics.</ref> When [[Smasher (Marvel Comics)|Smasher]] comes to the island, Forson has Jude the Entropic Man transform her into his messenger.<ref>''Avengers World'' #2 (January 2014). Marvel Comics.</ref>

Forson is an experienced scientist with genius-level intellect.

==In other media==
===Television===
* The Lyle Getz incarnation of [[Advanced Idea Mechanics|A.I.M.]]'s Scientist Supreme appears in the ''[[Iron Man: Armored Adventures]]'' animated series. In the episode "Ready, A.I.M., Fire", he assists his fellow A.I.M. operatives in building [[MODOK]]. In “Designed Only For Chaos”, A.I.M. uses the [[Living Laser]] to finish creating MODOK, who murders the Scientist Supreme so he can can take control of A.I.M. even though Getz was planning to do so anyway.
* Two different incarnations of A.I.M.'s Scientist Supreme appear in ''[[The Avengers: Earth's Mightiest Heroes]]'' animated series, voiced by [[Nolan North]] (first iteration),<ref>{{cite web|title=Full cast and crew of ''The Avengers: Earth's Mightiest Heroes'' "Breakout: Part 1|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1699027fullcredits#cast |work=[[IMDb]]|accessdate=2011-12-01}}</ref> and by [[Kyle Hebert]] (Lyle Getz).<ref>{{cite web|title=Full cast and crew of ''The Avengers: Earth's Mightiest Heroes'' "Prisoner of War|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2355209fullcredits#cast |work=[[IMDb]]|accessdate=2012-01-07}}</ref> In the episode "Breakout Pt. 1", an unidentified Scientist Supreme negotiates with [[Doctor Doom]]'s agent [[Lucia von Bardas]], before [[Iron Man]] intervenes and captures the A.I.M. agents. They are transferred to the [[Vault (comics)|Vault]], though they later escape off-screen. In "Alone Against A.I.M", the Scientist Supreme attempts to get revenge via the [[Technovore]], but he is defeated by the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]]. In "Prisoner of War", Getz is captured and replaced by a [[Skrull]] infiltrator before he joins forces with [[Captain America]] and his fellow prisoners to escape. In "Secret Invasion", Skrull Queen [[Veranke]] orders the Skrull impersonating Getz to use satellite weapons to destroy all life on Earth in response to the Avengers foiling her invasion, but [[Thor (Marvel Comics)|Thor]] destroys the satellites while the Skrulls are arrested by [[S.W.O.R.D. (comics)|S.W.O.R.D.]]
* An amalgamated iteration of A.I.M.'s Scientist Supreme appears in the ''[[Avengers Assemble (TV series)|Avengers: Ultron Revolution]]'' animated series episode "Adapting to Change", voiced by [[Jim Meskimen]].<ref>{{cite episode|title=Adapting to Change|series=Avengers: Ultron Revolution|serieslink=Avengers Assemble (TV series)|network=Disney XD|season=3|number=1|airdate=March 13, 2016}}</ref> This version has George Clinton's appearance, Valdemar Tykkio's accent, and Andrew Forson's attire. The Scientist Supreme uses three [[Super-Adaptoid|Adaptoids]] to fight the Avengers before merging with them to become the '''Supreme Adaptoid''', only to be defeated by the heroes and absorbed by [[Ultron]].
* The Monica Rappaccini incarnation of A.I.M.'s Scientist Supreme appears in the ''[[Spider-Man (2017 TV series)|Spider-Man]]'' animated series, voiced by [[Grey DeLisle]].<ref>{{cite episode|title=School of Hard Knocks|series=Spider-Man|network=Disney XD|season=2|number=30|airdate=July 9, 2018}}</ref>
