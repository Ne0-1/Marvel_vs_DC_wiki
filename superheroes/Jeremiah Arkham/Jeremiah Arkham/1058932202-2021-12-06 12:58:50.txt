'''Jeremiah Arkham''' is a [[fiction]]al [[supervillain]] and the head of the [[Arkham Asylum]] in [[DC Comics]], created by [[Alan Grant (writer)|Alan Grant]]. Arkham was created in 1992 and became the second villain to take up the mantle of the [[Black Mask (character)|Black Mask]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=22 March 2017|title=15 Things You Need To Know About Black Mask|url=https://screenrant.com/black-mask-backstory-explained-gotham-city-sirens-harley-quinn/|access-date=27 July 2021|website=ScreenRant|language=en-US}}</ref> Arkham slowly "lost his mind" during his time in the [[Arkham Asylum|Asylum]], subsequently becoming the second [[Black Mask (character)|Black Mask]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Black Mask II (Dr. Jeremiah Arkham {{!}} Prime Earth) (Comic Book Character)|url=https://comicbookrealm.com/report/character/7471/black-mask-ii-dr-jeremiah-arkham&t=indexing-history|access-date=27 July 2021|website=Comic Book Realm}}</ref> Though described as a sadist,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Jeremiah Arkham (Character)|url=https://comicvine.gamespot.com/jeremiah-arkham/4005-27541/|access-date=27 July 2021|website=Comic Vine|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Black Mask II (Dr. Jeremiah Arkham {{!}} Prime Earth) (Comic Book Character)|url=https://comicbookrealm.com/report/character/7471/black-mask-ii-dr-jeremiah-arkham&t=indexing-history|access-date=27 July 2021|website=Comic Book Realm}}</ref> Arkham seems to believe his intentions are for the benefit of his patients.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Jeremiah Arkham (Character)|url=https://comicvine.gamespot.com/jeremiah-arkham/4005-27541/|access-date=27 July 2021|website=Comic Vine|language=en}}</ref>

== History and origin ==
Arkham's [[Black Mask (character)|Black Mask]] is still canon and has been 'floating around' since the early 2000s, visible on the "Batman Universe" podcast. Although remaining canon, Jeremiah isn't usually the Black Mask used in films and media, including the [[Batman: Arkham Asylum|Arkham]] game series where he wasn't mentioned. Jeremiah's obscurity has been his downfall, with his latest comic appearance in [[DC Rebirth|DC's rebirth]] in 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Yumpu.com|title=pdf-download-dc-rebirth-omnibus-expanded-edition-full-online-by-geoff-johns|url=https://www.yumpu.com/xx/document/view/64318883/pdf-download-dc-rebirth-omnibus-expanded-edition-full-online-by-geoff-johns|access-date=7 July 2021|website=yumpu.com|language=en}}</ref> Jeremiah inherited the Arkham asylum from his late uncle [[Amadeus Arkham]], who named the asylum in wake of his dead mother, [[Elizabeth Arkham Asylum for the Criminally Insane|Elizabeth Arkham]].<ref> {{Cite web|title=Elizabeth Arkham (Character)|url=https://comicvine.gamespot.com/elizabeth-arkham/4005-87668/|access-date=7 July 2021|website=Comic Vine|language=en}}</ref> It's also believed to be named after the fictional [[Arkham massachusetts|Arkham]], [[Arkham massachusetts|Massachusetts]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Arkham: Lovecraft & Batman|url=http://arkhamcomics.weebly.com/arkham-lovecraft--batman.html|access-date=7 July 2021|website=Arkham Comics & Used Books|language=en}}</ref> Jeremiah was driven mad by the belief he could cure every patient, stemming from his teenage years when he talked a gunman down.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Doctor Arkham (Jeremiah Arkham) (Comic Book Character)|url=https://comicbookrealm.com/report/character/3504/dr.+arkham|access-date=2021-07-28|website=Comic Book Realm}}</ref> This 'savior complex' lead to trouble in his adulthood when he attempted to send his prisoners to attack the [[Batman]], after admitting himself to the hospital to stop the criminal [[Victor Zsasz]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-03-16|title=BATMAN: SHADOW OF THE BAT web|last=Noble|first=Barnes &|title=Batman: Shadow of the Bat Vol. 1 (NOOK Comics with Zoom View){{!}}NOOK by a contractor for a bribe,<ref>{{Cite web|title=CBUB Profile: Zsasz|url=https://www.electricferret.com/cbub/cbubcats/show/?cid=587|access-date=2021-07-28|website=www.electricferret.com}}</ref> and === Creative history ===
Arkham was created by [[Alan Grant (writer)|Alan Grant]] and [[Norm Breyfogle]] and first appeared in ''[[Batman: Shadow of the Bat|Shadow of the Bat #1]]'' in 1992.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Character: Jeremiah Arkham; psychologists (12923)|url=http://www.comicbookreligion.com/?c=12923&Jeremiah_Arkham|access-date=27 July 2021|website=www.comicbookreligion.com}}</ref> In his first appearance, [[Batman]] was committed to the [[Arkham Asylum|asylum]] for allegedly killing a police officer.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Batman: Shadow of the Bat #2 - The Last Arkham: Part Two (Issue)|url=https://comicvine.gamespot.com/batman-shadow-of-the-bat-2-the-last-arkham-part-tw/4000-35943/|access-date=2021-07-31|website=Comic Vine|language=en}}</ref> In the issue, Dr. Arkham is shown to believe he can convince the [[Batman]] to willingly reveal his identity to him.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Batman: Shadow of the Bat Vol. 1 – Comics by comiXology|url=https://www.comixology.com/Batman-Shadow-of-the-Bat-Vol-1/digital-comic/379410?ref=c2VhcmNoL2luZGV4L2Rlc2t0b3Avc2xpZGVyTGlzdC90b3BSZXN1bHRzU2xpZGVy|access-date=9 July 2021|website=www.comixology.com}}</ref> After ''[[Batman: No Man's Land]]'', ''[[Batman: Shadow of the Bat|Shadow of the Bat]]'' was replaced by ''[[Batman: Gotham Knights]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Batman: Shadow of the Bat (Volume)|url=https://comicvine.gamespot.com/batman-shadow-of-the-bat/4050-4724/|access-date=27 July 2021|website=Comic Vine|language=en}}</ref> In the course of about two-hundred comics, Jeremiah's original character was [[Flanderization|flanderized]], turning from a compassionate doctor who believed he could [[cure]] any patient to an "obsessed manic wreck".<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|title=Jeremiah Arkham (Character)|url=https://comicvine.gamespot.com/jeremiah-arkham/4005-27541/|access-date=9 July 2021|website=Comic Vine|language=en}}</ref> In a reference to his childhood, [[the Joker]] held a gun to Arkham and forced him to watch the inmates leave the asylum after an attack from [[Bane (DC Comics)|Bane]], while the [[Joker (character)|Joker]] tried to scare the administrator into madness (later saved by [[Batman]]).

== Related characters ==
=== Enemies ===
[[Catwoman]] – [[Catwoman]] would eventually kill Jeremiah. 

[[Erasmus Rayne|Death Rattle]] – [[Erasmus Rayne|Death Rattle]] is a cult-leader locked in [[Arkham Asylum|Arkham]] and an alleged psychic. [[Erasmus Rayne|Death Rattle]] founded his own religion centered around his cult and is a spiritism cult leader. [[Erasmus Rayne|Erasmus]] believed he could communicate with the dead, suggested by his name as a play on the death rattle phenomena. He disliked Jeremiah due to his position as warden. 

[[Jason Todd]] – Along with [[Dick Grayson]], [[Jason Todd]] worked for [[Batman]] and developed a distaste for Jeremiah, especially while Jeremiah took up the mantle of [[Black Mask (character)|Black Mask]].

[[Victor Zsasz]] – Along with [[Erasmus Rayne|Erasmus]], [[Zsasz]] initially seemed indifferent to Jeremiah, speaking about a "disease" his uncle contracted. After Jeremiah became [[Black Mask (character)|Black Mask]], however, Zsasz became a natural enemy for Jeremiah. 

[[Poison Ivy (character)|Poison Ivy]] – [[Poison Ivy (character)|Poison Ivy]] was a regular inmate for [[Arkham Asylum]], leading to her rivalry with its head, Jeremiah Arkham. 

[[Joker (character)|The Joker]] – [[Joker (character)|Joker]] attempted to destabilize Jeremiah's psyche and cause him to become insane.

[[Victor Zehrhard]] – [[Victor Zehrhard|Victor]] was the warden of the rivaling [[Blackgate Penitentiary]].

=== Allies  ===
[[Aaron Cash]] – [[Aaron Cash]] is a guard at [[Arkham Asylum]] who's frequently appeared in stories and comics. 

[[Amadeus Arkham]] – [[Amadeus Arkham|Amadeus]] had a good relationship with his [[newphew]] before his death.

[[Wild (DC)|Wild]] – [[Wild (DC)|Wild]]'s family was murdered by the [[Joker (character)|Joker]] leading him on a path of vengeance as a vigilante.

[[Wyndham Vane]] – [[Wyndham Vane]] was a psychiatrist at [[Arkham Asylum|Arkham]] who performed dangerous therapy without authorization. 

=== Other  ===
[[Batman]] – [[Batman|The Batman]] repeatedly stopped Jeremiah and admitted him to [[Arkham Asylum|Arkham]], although [[Batman]] seems to think Jeremiah is ill, despite worsening it at times. As such, [[Batman]] has tried to help the doctor. 

[[Dick Grayson]] – [[Dick Grayson|Dick]] was one of the characters who took up the mantle of [[Robin (character)|Robin]] and worked alongside the [[Batman]], who believed Jeremiah was insane. As such, [[Robin (character)|Grayson]] helped [[Batman]] thwart Jeremiah multiple times. Despite this, [[Dick Grayson|Dick]] shares his goal of helping the doctor.

[[Hamburger Lady (DC)|Hamburger Lady]] – [[Hamburger Lady (DC)|Hamburger Lady]] was part of a series of inmates imagined by Jeremiah.

[[No Face (DC)|No Face]] – [[No Face (DC)|No Face]] was a part of a series of inmates imagined by Jeremiah. 

[[Solly Bean]] – [[Solly Bean]] is a cannibal in [[Arkham Asylum|arkham]] whose urges are stopped by his medication. When [[Medication|medicated]], he's friendly with the other inmates and guards. [[Solly Bean|Bean]] also has clinical depression treated by antidepressant pills.

[[Hugo Strange]] – [[Hugo Strange|Strange]] was a psychiatrist at [[Arkham Asylum]] with Jeremiah, although their professional relationship was never mentioned 

== Comics ==
=== Shadow of the Bat / Batman: The Last Arkham ===
''[[Batman: Shadow of the Bat|Shadow of the Bat]]'' is the first appearance of Jeremiah.<ref name="auto"/> In the story, a contractor releases [[Victor Zsasz]], enabling [[Victor Zsasz|Zsasz's]] murder spree shortly after.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Victor Zsasz|url=https://www.dcuniverseinfinite.com/encyclopedia/victor-zsasz/|access-date=2021-07-31|website=www.dcuniverseinfinite.com}}</ref> When the [[Batman]] discovers the bodies, he recognizes [[Victor Zsasz|Zsasz's]] killing pattern, although it was known that [[Victor Zsasz|Zsasz]] was in the [[Arkham Asylum|Asylum]]. Eventually, [[Batman]] finds the escape the contractor left [[Victor Zsasz|Victor]] and captures him.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Batman: Shadow of the Bat #4 Reviews|url=https://leagueofcomicgeeks.com/comic/2713315/batman-shadow-of-the-bat-4|access-date=2021-07-31|website=League of Comic Geeks}}</ref> When Jeremiah discovers [[Batman]] had not only feigned insanity, but also stopped [[Zsasz]]'s 'exposure therapy', he is enraged and releases patients on [[Batman]], most notably [[Amygdala (character)|Amygdala]] (who Jeremiah was manipulating) and [[Riddler]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Amygdala (Character)|url=https://comicvine.gamespot.com/amygdala/4005-25089/|access-date=2021-07-31|website=Comic Vine|language=en}}</ref> 

=== Batman: Knightfall ===
In ''[[Batman: Knightfall|Knightfall]]'', [[Bane (DC Comics)|Bane]] breaks into [[Arkham Asylum]] and frees the inmates as [[the Joker]] holds Jeremiah at gunpoint. [[Joker (character)|Joker]] drives Jeremiah to near insanity until the [[Batman]] saves him.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Black Mask II (Dr. Jeremiah Arkham {{!}} Prime Earth) (Comic Book Character)|url=https://comicbookrealm.com/report/character/7471/black-mask-ii-dr-jeremiah-arkham&t=indexing-history|access-date=2021-07-31|website=Comic Book Realm}}</ref> 

=== No Man's Land ===
In ''[[Batman: No Man's Land]]'', Jeremiah heads to the ruins of [[Arkham Asylum|Arkham]] and finds his late uncle's journal. Jeremiah decides to rebuild [[Arkham Asylum]] in his uncle's image. While the [[Arkham Asylum|asylum]] is finishing renovations, workplace accidents begin to pile up, including [[Killer Croc]]'s tank filtration rupturing, [[Mr. Freeze]]'s room gets warmer, along with the [[Raggedy Man (disambiguation)|Raggedy Man]] being freed. Later on, [[Alyce Sinner]] is shown to be having sexual relations with [[Black Mask (character)|Black Mask (Roman Sionis)]]. [[Alyce Sinner|Alyce]] then returns to the asylum and projects [[Amadeus Arkham]]'s voice to incite a riot with the inmates.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alyce Sinner (Character)|url=https://comicvine.gamespot.com/alyce-sinner/4005-71931/|access-date=2021-07-31|website=Comic Vine|language=en}}</ref> After [[Batman]] returns the inmates to their cells, [[Raggedy Man (disambiguation)|Raggedy Man]] is then found dead, which upsets Jeremiah deeply. Later, it's revealed that this is part of the [[Black Mask (character)|Black Mask's]] plan to defeat the warden of Arkham. Afterwards, the [[Prison warden|warden]] is put into a cell and it's revealed that his personal patients ([[Hamburger Lady (DC)|Hamburger Lady]], [[No Face (DC)|No Face]], etc.) were all figments of his imaginations.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Three Beauties (Team)|url=https://comicvine.gamespot.com/three-beauties/4060-58171/|access-date=2021-07-31|website=Comic Vine|language=en}}</ref>

=== DC Rebirth ===
In [[DC Rebirth]], Jeremiah at one point had sexual relations with Dr. [[List of minor DC Comics characters|Ingrid Karlsson]] who gave birth to their child, then [[List of minor DC Comics characters|Karlsson]] died. To keep his daughter ([[Astrid Arkham]]) safe, Jeremiah raised her within the walls of [[Arkham Asylum|Arkham]], where she developed a hatred for the [[Batman]], and she later became the [[Arkham Knight]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Arkham Knight (Character)|url=https://comicvine.gamespot.com/arkham-knight/4005-156487/|access-date=2021-07-31|website=Comic Vine|language=en}}</ref>

=== Battle for the Cowl ===
In [[Batman: Battle for the Cowl|Battle for the Cowl]], Arkham is causing mass-destruction in [[Gotham City|Gotham]] even [[Arson|exploding]] [[Arkham Asylum]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Battle for the Cowl (Story Arc)|url=https://comicvine.gamespot.com/battle-for-the-cowl/4045-55808/|access-date=2021-08-18|website=Comic Vine|language=en}}</ref> This is his first appearance as [[Black Mask (character)|Black Mask]].
